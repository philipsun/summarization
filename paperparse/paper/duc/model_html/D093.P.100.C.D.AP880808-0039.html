[1] Overcrowding and safety violations caused an aging ferry to capsize in the Ganges River. [2] The ferry accident was said to be India's worst ever. [3] The ferry was overloaded with people and wheat and lacked life jackets. [4] Authorities believed at least 300 people on the lower deck died and about 100 swam to safety. [5] The boat sank a short time after it sailed about 100 yards from shore. [6] The boat trip is normally used by pilgrims crossing the river to worship at a Hindu shrine. [7] Boat accidents are common during the monsoon season when rivers overflow their banks.
