[1] There were several shining moments for US Olympians in the 1988 Summer Games. [2] Diver Greg Louganis won a second pair of gold medals after a springboard accident, track star Florence Griffith Joyner won three golds and a silver medal, and her sister-in-law, Jackie Joiner-Kersee won two golds. [3] Canadian Ben Johnson had rocketed to a world record performance over US sprinter Carl Lewis in the 100 meter but was stripped of the gold medal when he tested positive for anabolic steroid use. [4] The Games also saw increasing anti-American sentiment by the host Koreans because of perceived rudeness of the US team and NBC coverage of events.
