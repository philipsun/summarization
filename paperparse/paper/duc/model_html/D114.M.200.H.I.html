[1] Fighting has broken out sporadically in the Indian state of Jammu-Kashmir since January 20. [2] At least 1,034 people had been killed by late August. [3] The Indian government is battling Muslim insurgents seeking the formation of an independent, neutral, nation. [4] India (predominately Hindu) and Pakistan (predominately Islamic) have fought two wars over Kashmir since the nations were established in 1947. [5] Jammu-Kashmir is 62 percent Muslim. [6] Insurgent tactics have included bombings, kidnappings, killings of suspected police informers, ambushes of police vehicles, and attacks on police installations. [7] Large demonstrations have been held in Jammu-Kashmir and on the Pakistani border. [8] Indian tactics have include retaliatory fire, arresting and killing insurgent leaders, 24-hour curfews, disrupting and firing on demonstrations, and interfering with funerals of dead insurgents. [9] The state is now governed directly from New Delhi. [10] In August, India increased border forces and operations and killed 80 infiltrators, claiming the insurgents are being trained and armed in Pakistan. [11] Pakistan denies these charges but says it does support the Muslims' crusade for "self-determination". [12] The Prime Minister of Pakistan has sought the support of other Islamic nations. [13] The two nations have exchanged fire on several occasions, but both say there is no cause for concern.
