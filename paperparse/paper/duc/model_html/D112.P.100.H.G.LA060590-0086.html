[1] British media entrepreneur Robert Maxwell, who publishes Britain's Daily Mirror, said Monday he is involved in negotiations to buy a U.S. newspaper worth "hundreds of millions of dollars". [2] The newspaper was not identified but there is speculation that it might be The New York Daily News, which is struggling to restore profitability; USA Today, which would mesh with Maxwell's efforts for the new paper The European; or the Chicago Sun-Times that has been sliding in circulation since 1985. [3] Officials of each of these newspapers denied they were negotiating with Maxwell, who recently bought three US tabloids: the Globe, National Examiner, and the Sun.
