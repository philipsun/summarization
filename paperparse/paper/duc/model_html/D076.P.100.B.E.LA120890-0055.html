[1] Queen Elizabeth awarded former Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher the Order of Merit on Friday. [2] The award follows quickly on the heels of Mrs. [3] Thatcher's resignation as prime minister. [4] The quickness of the award suggests that the Queen wished to show that there was no animosity between the two women. [5] At the same time, Mrs. [6] Thatcher's husband, Denis, was awarded the hereditary knighthood of baronet. [7] He will be formally addressed as Sir Denis and his wife as Lady Thatcher, although she insists she wishes to be known simply as Mrs. Thatcher. [8] The Order of Merit, created in 1902, is restricted to only 24 men and women.
