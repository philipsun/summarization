[1] The death toll from floods in eastern Ohio rose to 21 and may to higher since 14 persons are still missing. [2] Torrential rains caused two creeks near Shadyside, Ohio, overflow and create a wall of water 15 to 20 feet high. [3] The flood raged through the valley demolishing a crowded tavern and at least 70 houses. [4] Governor Celeste declared a state of emergency and sent National Guard troops into the area to assist. [5] Dog teams were used to search for survivors and bodies. [6] Few of the damaged homes were covered by flood insurance so the owners must look to federal assistance.
