[1] American Insurance Service Group says Hurricane Hugo, which ravaged the Caribbean and the Carolinas last week, is responsible for record hurricane losses. [2] State Farm Insurance expects claims of approximately $500 million in the Carolinas. [3] Nationwide Insurance is projecting claims near $100 million. [4] Though the Hugo-related losses are substantial, most companies have sufficient funds to cover them. [5] Some companies, however, will reduce third-quarter earnings. [6] Hugo slammed into Charleston Thursday night with top winds of 135 mph. [7] Upgraded to a Category 4 hurricane hours before it struck the historic city, it caused widespread damage throughout the Carolinas. [8] Only two lives were lost in Charleston, however, and only 17 total in South Carolina. [9] Officials credit the low number of fatalities to residents who monitored the hurricane and evacuated. [10] Computer models at the National Hurricane Center used satellite information to track Hugo's path into Charleston. [11] Before the storm's arrival in South Carolina, the governor declared a state of emergency and National Guardsmen were ordered to assist a mandatory evacuation of coastal barrier islands and shorefront. [12] Thousands fled inland or went to shelters. [13] Kingston, New York, assisted by Charleston during the Revolutionary War, is repaying the kindness by sending money to help the city recover.
