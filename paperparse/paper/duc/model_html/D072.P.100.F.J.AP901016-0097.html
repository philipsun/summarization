[1] Brilliant and flamboyant Leonard Bernstein, the first internationally acclaimed American maestro, was buried today at Greenwood Cemetery in Brooklyn. [2] He died Sunday at 72 of lung failure. [3] Flags at Lincoln Center, home of the New York Philharmonic which Bernstein conducted from 1958 to 1969, were lowered in his memory. [4] The Philharmonic will perform an all Bernstein program beginning Thursday and continuing through Oct. 23. [5] A memorial concert is also planned for Nov. 14, the 47th anniversary of Bernstein's Philharmonic debut. [6] In addition to conducting and composing, Bernstein introduced thousands to classical music through his Emmy-winning television program, "Young People's Concerts".
