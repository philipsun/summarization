[1] Andrei Sakharov helped develop the Soviet hydrogen bomb but urged its limitation. [2] He was a prominent 1970s dissident and won the 1975 Nobel Peace Prize. [3] In January 1980, after criticizing Soviet intervention in Afghanistan, Breshnev exiled him to Gorky. [4] Gorbachev allowed Sakharov and his wife Yelena Bonner's return to Moscow in December 1986. [5] Their apartment became a mecca for aid-seekers. [6] Sakharov supported Gorbachev's reforms but urged improvement in human rights and a nuclear testing ban. [7] In April 1989 he was elected to parliament after Soviet physicists voted secretly to nominate him. [8] Sakharov, 68, died in December 1989 of an apparent heart attack after urging creation of an opposition party.
