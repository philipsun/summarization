[1] The longest criminal trial in U.S. history, the McMartin Pre-School molestation case, began in August 1983 with a telephone call to the Manhattan Beach police. [2] This began a series of events which led to the arrest of Raymond Buckey, his mother Peggy McMartin Buckey, his sister Peggy Ann Buckey, his grandmother Virginia McMartin and three employees, Mary Ann Jackson, Babette Spitter, and Betty Raidor. [3] Charges were dropped against all by Buckey and his mother in January 1986 and the trial of the remaining two began in April 1987. [4] The case finally ended on January 18 1990 when the were acquitted.
