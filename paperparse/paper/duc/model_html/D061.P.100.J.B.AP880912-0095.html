[1] Hurricane Gilbert, packing 110mph winds and torrential rain, moved over the Jamaican capital city of Kingston today after skirting Puerto Rico, Haiti and the Dominican Republic. [2] It's tropical-storm force winds extend up to 250 miles to the north and 100 miles to the south. [3] Hal Gerrish, a forecaster with the National Hurricane Center said it is one of the larger systems seen in the Caribbean for a long time. [4] Warnings were posted for the Cayman Islands, Haiti and Cuba but discontinued for the Dominican Republic. [5] Jamaicans are expecting to bear the brunt of Gilbert as its eye moves lengthwise across the island.
