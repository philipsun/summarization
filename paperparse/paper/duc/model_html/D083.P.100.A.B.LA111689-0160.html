[1] In Huntsville, Ala., a tornado slammed into a shopping district, a residential area and a school Wednesday, killing at least 14 people, injuring nearly 300 and trapping many others in their cars and homes. [2] It touched down between 4 and 4:30 p.m., when city streets were filled with rush-hour traffic. [3] One witness said that it started picking up cars and strewing them around... heavy overhead power lines started snapping just like a whip. [4] Telephone communication and electricity were disrupted. [5] Only one radio station remained broadcasting. [6] Rescuers are digging in the rubble. [7] State police were called from around the state to help.
