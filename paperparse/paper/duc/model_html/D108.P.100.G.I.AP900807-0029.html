[1] Iranian editorial writers are rejoicing that the rest of the world is waking up to one of their most hated bogeymen--Saddam Hussein. [2] Saddam attacked Iran in September 1980 and the war dragged on until August 1988. [3] For the last six months, editorial writers have backed off Saddam because of U.N. sponsored bilateral talks. [4] The invasion of Kuwait changed this. [5] The writers now are castigating Kuwait and the United States--the Great Satan--for creating a Frankenstein by supporting Iraq in the war. [6] Some Iranian pundits believe this will be an excuse for the United States to reenter the Persian Gulf.
