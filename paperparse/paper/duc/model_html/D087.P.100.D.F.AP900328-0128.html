[1] The late Jesse Owens, hero of the 1936 Berlin Olympics where he exploded the Hitler myth of Aryan superiority, was honored at a White House ceremony when President Bush presented the Congressional Gold Medal to his widow, Ruth Owens. [2] Bush cited Owens' four gold medals at the Berlin Games as "a victory for humanity". [3] Bush, borrowing a phrase from Ruth Owens, said it was not the athletic feats but "his humanitarian contributions in the race of life" that earned the medal for Owens. [4] Owens' name lives on through many activities and a foundation that awards scholarships to needy youth.
