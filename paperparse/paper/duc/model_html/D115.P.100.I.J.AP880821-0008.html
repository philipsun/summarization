[1] A strong earthquake hit Nepal and parts of eastern India at 4:54 a.m. Sunday and, according to first reports, 100 people may have died. [2] Nepal's Department of Geology said the quake, centered near the border town of Udayapur, Nepal, measured 5.7 on the Richter scale and lasted approximately 40 seconds. [3] In New Delhi, the Seismological Observatory said the quake measured 6.5 on the Richter scale. [4] Radio Nepal said houses collapsed in Bhaktapur and Dharan killing at least 27. [5] In the Indian city of Dharbhanga, Bihar, 14 children are believed dead after their school hostel collapsed.
