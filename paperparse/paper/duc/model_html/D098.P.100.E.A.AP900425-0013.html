[1] The long delayed launching of the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) has evoked a burst of superlatives from the nation's scientists. [2] From James Westphal at the California Institute of Technology we hear, "No one ever made anything that good". [3] Edward Weiler, the chief NASA scientist on the HST project declares, "On a clear, dark night you can see a flashlight from two miles away. [4] With a 28-power telescope you can see it on the moon 250,000 miles away. [5] With the HST you could see a firefly in Australia from Washington". [6] Others hail HST as the greatest thing since Galileo's telescope in 1609.
