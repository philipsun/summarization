[1] Ousted East German leader Erich Honecker was arrested and taken to prison after his release from a hospital where he underwent surgery for a kidney tumor. [2] He is to be tried for high treason in March, along with former Politburo members Erich Mielke, Guenter Mittag, and Joachim Hermann. [3] Eleven other ousted members are in prison awaiting trial. [4] East Germany has been in a frenzy to root out corruption. [5] Honecker could be sentenced to a maximum of life imprisonment. [6] His doctors say that he is too ill to stay in jail. [7] East Germany's Lutheran Church has offered to place him in a home for the aged.
