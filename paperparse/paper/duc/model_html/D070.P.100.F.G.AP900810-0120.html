[1] East German authorities on Friday interrogated former Communist leader Erich Honecker about his role in the killing of some 200 people trying to escape from East Germany's Communist rule. [2] Honecker, would be investigated on possible murder charges in connection with those deaths. [3] Along with several other members of his ousted regime, he already has been charged with corruption and abuse of power. [4] The murder charge could put Honecker away for life. [5] His poor health and his age, 77, make prosecution questionable. [6] He has been judged too ill for imprisonment but fit enough for prosecution. [7] He is staying in a hospital outside East Berlin.
