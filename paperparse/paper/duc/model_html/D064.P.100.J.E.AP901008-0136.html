[1] McDonald's opened their first restaurant in China on Monday the in city of Shenzhen. [2] China has long been known for its cuisine but most Chinese workers, who average $32.00 a month, are unable to afford fancy restaurants. [3] Even the higher wages of Shenzhen, where workers average $53.00 a month, the $1.14 cost of the Big Mac along with 48 cents for fries and 74 cents for a shake may cause the Chinese to view eating at McDonald's as a treat rather than a convenience. [4] Such is the case with Kentucky Fried Chicken, which opened its first restaurant in Peking three years ago. [5] While popular, the Chinese still consider eating there a treat.
