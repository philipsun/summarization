[1] Scientists do not know what fuels a budding hurricane as development begins. [2] It is a mysterious convergence of winds, moisture and temperature near the equator. [3] About one out of six of the 50 to 60 low-pressure troughs moving west out of Africa each year develop into a higher level storm. [4] A tropical trough, or wave, becomes a depression when its winds start swirling. [5] When sustained winds reach 39mph, the system becomes a named tropical storm. [6] It reaches hurricane status when sustained winds hit 74mph. [7] Scientists believe that hurricanes' primary role is to transport heat from the lower to the upper atmosphere.
