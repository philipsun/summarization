[1] Cassettes and film footage collected by Yoko Ono constituted the main elements of the movie Imagine: John Lennon. [2] The items Ono provided were edited to make the late John Lennon the narrator of the much acclaimed film. [3] Despite her intimacy with the material, Ono found it painful to watch the film. [4] The movie captures the music and times in which Lennon lived but doesn't delve deeply into the Beatles' drug use and abusive tempers. [5] Ono says that there is still enough footage for another movie. [6] Also, Lennon had several unpublished songs, never studio recorded, which might become a record or possibly a musical.
