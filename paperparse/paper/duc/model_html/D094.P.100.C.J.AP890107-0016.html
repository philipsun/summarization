[1] Emperor Hirohito, 87, will be remembered for his first effective and the most courageous decision during his 62-year reign: Japan must surrender and end the Pacific conflict in WW II. [2] He is generally portrayed as having played no significant role in Japan's war efforts. [3] In the first 20 years of his reign, Hirohito was worshipped as a divinity. [4] On January 1, 1945 he publicly renounced his divinity, enabling him to remain as the symbolic head of Japan and assuring that he would not face charges as a war criminal. [5] In post-WW II Japan, he was often viewed as a kind uncle.
