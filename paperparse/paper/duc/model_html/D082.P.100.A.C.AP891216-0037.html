[1] Sakharov's obituary was signed by President Gorbachev, five other politburo members, and fifty other leading officials and scholars. [2] It was published in Pravda. [3] Such obituaries are reserved for favored public officials, and Pravda emphasized Sakharov's importance by printing the decree on the funeral commission on the front page. [4] The obituary praised Sakharov as a man of honesty and sincerity. [5] This amounted to a public apology for the 1989 exile ordered by President Brezhnev. [6] Gorbachev now blames Brezhnev for allowing stagnation and corruption to overcome the Soviet system. [7] Gorbachev called Sakharov in Gorky in December1986 and ended the exile.
