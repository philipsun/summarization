[1] As protests gathered strength in the fall of 1989, East Germany's longtime orthodox leader, Erich Honecker "lost touch with reality" according to the man who succeeded him as Communist leader only to be ousted later. [2] Egon Krenz, the successor and former Politburo member Guenter Schabowski gave their versions of the bunker mentality that engulfed Honecker. [3] A commission is probing possible criminal charges stemming from police brutality during demonstrations by dissident citizens in October 1989. [4] Honecker was driven from office 10 days later. [5] Krenz, who was thrown out 6 weeks after succeeding Honecker, said he knew nothing about any orders to shoot demonstrators.
