[1] President Bush named career diplomat Deane Hinton as ambassador to Panama as a recess appointment since Congress is not in session. [2] Hinton, currently ambassador to Costa Rica, replaces Arthur Davis who had been recalled in protest of what the administration considered the stealing of the Panamanian elections by General Manuel Noriega. [3] Davis was later returned to Panama after US forces invaded Panama and Guillermo Endara was installed as president. [4] Hinton has also been ambassador to El Salvador and Pakistan. [5] Senate Majority Leader George Mitchell called Hinton highly qualified because of his "wide-ranging experience and expertise in Central America".
