[1] Robert Ballard, an American oceanographer, earlier this month used an ocean-crawling robot to locate the German battleship Bismarck. [2] The battleship is resting under 15,000 feet of water about 600 miles west of Brest, France. [3] It went down on 27 May 1941. [4] Only 110 of the 2,200-man crew were survived the sinking of Germany's most powerful battleship. [5] Structural evidence convinced Ballard the Germans scuttled the battleship. [6] Only scuttled ships tend to remain in one piece. [7] Under the laws of the sea the battleship is still German property. [8] Ballard said that he would reveal the exact location only to Germany.
