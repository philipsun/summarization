[1] After 7 years of negotiation with the South Korean government, McDonald's opened the first of what it hopes to be a string of restaurants in Seoul. [2] Opened by McAhn Industries, a 50-50 joint venture of McDonald's and accountant-turned-entrepreneur, Ahn Hyo Young, McDonald's struggled with the government over tight import restrictions and McAhn's royalty payments to the U.S. [3] The import question was settled by finding a local supplier of high quality beef and potatoes at a value. [4] With a growing middle class, Korea may be McDonald's most promising foreign market. [5] Expected to benefit from the 1988 Olympic Games, McDonald's strategy is to take things slowly and avoid extravagance.
