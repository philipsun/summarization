[1] Leonard Bernstein, the Renaissance man, died Sunday at 72 from lung disease. [2] He had remarkable talent and expertise in conducting, composing, playing the piano, and teaching, which he combined with a unique theatrical flair in both his professional and private lives. [3] He was born of Russian Jewish immigrants, began the piano at age 10, and was educated at Boston Latin School and Harvard University. [4] He was mentored by the great musicians of the era. [5] Perhaps his greatest successes were conducting the New York Philharmonic and composing "West Side Story" for Broadway. [6] He was a heavy smoker and drinker with an uproarious, liberal, life style.
