[1] Indiana Senator Dan Quayle, 41, George Bush's surprise choice for vice-president in 1988, was criticized as a novice. [2] His National Guard service and campaign gaffes were attacked. [3] He proved a low-profile team player and Bush announced he would be on the 1992 ticket. [4] Quayle's grandfather owned the newspaper in Quayle's hometown of Huntington. [5] Quayle, known always as a nice guy, graduated from DePauw in 1969, took National Guard training, then in 1971 worked in the Indiana Attorney General's office and for the governor. [6] At Indiana University Law School he met and married his wife Marilyn. [7] They practiced law together before Quayle spent 12 years in Congress. [8] They have three children.
