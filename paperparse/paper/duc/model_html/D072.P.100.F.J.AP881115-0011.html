[1] Leonard Bernstein, 70, returned to Carnegie Hall with the New York Philharmonic Monday, 45 years after his triumphant debut with the orchestra. [2] Three Bernstein compositions, all played stunningly, formed the program. [3] At intermission, Mrs. Arthur Rodzinski, widow of the Philharmonic music director who hired Bernstein as his assistant in 1943, was introduced. [4] Violinist Jacques Margolies, who played in that first concert, is still a member of the orchestra and played Monday night. [5] Eight others who were members in 1943 went on stage. [6] Bernstein, in his 1,215th appearance as Philharmonic conductor, received a silver box engraved with a reproduction of Monday evening's program.
