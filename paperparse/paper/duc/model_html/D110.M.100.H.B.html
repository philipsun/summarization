[1] Iraq, after accusing Kuwait of multiple, harmful wrongdoings, invaded and overran Kuwait. [2] It claimed to have responded to a request by a Kuwaiti revolutionary group. [3] A skeptical U.S. and U.N. rejected this justification, condemned the action, demanded withdrawal, and threatened sanctions. [4] The beleaguered Kuwaiti government requested U.S. military intervention. [5] Divided Arab nations failed to resolve the crisis. [6] After further Iraqi intransigence, strong U.N. economic and military sanctions were imposed, including a ban on Iraqi oil and imports. [7] The price of oil soared. [8] Iraq responded by rounding up foreigners in Kuwait, sending some to Baghdad, and alerting its citizens to a possible U.S. attack.
