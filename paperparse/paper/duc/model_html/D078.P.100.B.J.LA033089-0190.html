[1] At Wednesday's Academy Awards, "Rain Man" won four Oscars and "Dangerous Liaisons" and "Who Framed Roger Rabbit" each won three. [2] Seven other films also each won one award. [3] Ten movies split 17 awards, the most winners for one year this decade. [4] Refreshingly, several winners came from films that were not box office smashes: Jodie Foster as best actress ("The Accused"); Geena Davis as best supporting actress ("The Accidental Tourist"); sound editing for "Bird;" and David Grusin's score of "The Milagro Beanfield War". [5] The 1988 slate of films was a marked improvement over recent years and reflected an older, more demanding audience.
