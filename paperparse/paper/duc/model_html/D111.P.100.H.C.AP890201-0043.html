[1] The 400-foot Argentine supply ship, the Bahia Paraiso, sank Tuesday after running aground. [2] The ship was 600 miles south of Cape Horn, and about two miles from a United States research facility on the Antarctic Peninsula. [3] The 316 people aboard the ship, including 82 tourists, were rescued by power boats after the ship ran aground. [4] The ship was carrying jet fuel, gasoline, canisters of compressed gases, and other supplies to the Argentine Esperanza Station near the tip of the Antarctica Peninsula. [5] There is great concern that diesel fuel would leak and damage the unspoiled ecology of Antarctica.
