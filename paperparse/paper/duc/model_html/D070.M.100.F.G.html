[1] In October 1989, after being hospitalized for kidney surgery, former East German leader Erich Honecker--along with other Party officials--was arrested and imprisoned to await trial for treason and corruption. [2] Judged too ill from imprisonment, he was placed in a Soviet East Berlin hospital. [3] He also faced a murder charge about his role in killing people trying to escape from East Berlin. [4] In 1992, Honecker took refuge in the Chilean Embassy in Moscow but was soon ordered to leave. [5] In Germany, he was tried, imprisoned and released in 1993. [6] He died in Santiago, Chile in 1994 at age 81.
