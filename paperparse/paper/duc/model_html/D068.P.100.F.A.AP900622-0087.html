[1] Soviet Foreign Minister Shevardnadze proposed today that a united Germany be allowed to join NATO after a five-year period during which all Soviet and U.S. troops would leave the country. [2] U.S. Secretary of State Baker responded, "I am underwhelmed". [3] The United States objects to the 5-year waiting period while the Soviet Union has opposed a united Germany's membership in NATO. [4] Shevardnadze also remarked at a ceremony for the removal of Checkpoint Charlie that all western troops should be removed from "greater Berlin" within six months after unification, a provision which apparently would also apply to Soviet troops just outside the city proper.
