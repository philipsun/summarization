[1] The Boston Marathon is becoming a reckless speed duel and the last three have produced five of the top 10 times in the race's history. [2] They also have resulted in a race of attrition, with many of the early speedsters faltering in the late stages. [3] Last year's winner, Gelindo Bordin, ran a patient, calculating race, passing thee race leaders at the 21 mile mark and winning in 2 hours, 8 minutes, 19 seconds, the second fastest time in the race's history. [4] Foremost among the women entrants is Ingrid Kristiansen, who holds the world record of 2:21:06 and who won in Boston in 1986 and 1989.
