[1] A large family group was permitted to visit Nelson Mandela in prison on his 71st birthday. [2] Mandela's wife, Winnie, and several of his children, grandchildren, a daughter-in-law and a great-grandson comprised the group. [3] There had been speculation that Mandela would be released from prison, especially after a summer meeting with President P, W. Botha. [4] Mandela told his wife that the meeting should not be interpreted as leading to his release. [5] Mandela has been in prison since 1962 when he was sentenced to five years. [6] He was then given a life sentence in 1964 for sabotage and plotting to overthrow the government.
