[1] Since roaring to life about two weeks ago, explosions at Mount Pinatubo, located 10 miles from Clark AFB in the Philippines, have become increasingly severe. [2] The first eruptions shot gas, steam and ash 12,000 feet above the mountain. [3] Today's eruptions spewed matter over 12 miles high, and a possible lava flow was seen for the first time. [4] Clark, evacuated nearly a week ago, is covered in ash, as is Subic Bay naval base, which received the 16,000 Clark evacuees. [5] U.S. officials, while not commenting on whether nuclear weapons are at the abandoned base, denied the possibility of radioactive contamination if molten rock hits the weapons depots. [6] At least four deaths and 24 people injured have been recorded since Pinatubo began erupting after six centuries of dormancy. [7] About 84,000 have been evacuated. [8] Heavy rains today have raised fears of catastrophic mudflows. [9] Pinatubo is in the "Ring of Fire" that circles the Pacific. [10] Mount Unzen in Japan, also in the ring, recently erupted, killing 38. [11] President Aquino toured refugee centers and authorized the release of $1.42 million for relief efforts. [12] According to Foreign Secretary Manglapus, the eruptions should not effect the stalled talks with the U.S. on new Clark and Subic lease agreements.
