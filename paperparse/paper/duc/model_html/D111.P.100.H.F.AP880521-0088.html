[1] Greek divers have found the remains of a Turkish warship, the Bourloti Seimaz, in the eastern Aegean near the island of Chios. [2] Greek revolutionaries had sunk the ship in 1822 in revenge for Turkish killing and looting on Chios. [3] Thousands died in Chios and this action put European countries behind the Greek struggle for independence from the Ottoman Empire, which they won in 1830. [4] The Greeks had rammed the Turkish ship with a boatload of explosives. [5] The Greeks escaped the explosion that killed the Ottoman commander. [6] The remains of the ship lie under 162 feet of water making recovery difficult.
