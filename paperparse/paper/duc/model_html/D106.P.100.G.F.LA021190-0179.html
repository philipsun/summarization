[1] World leaders welcomed South Africa's announcement that Nelson Mandela would be released from prison. [2] Many of the world's nations had cut off commercial contact with South Africa because of its policy of apartheid. [3] Anti-apartheid foes warned against lessening pressure against South Africa even as they celebrated the announcement. [4] International support for anti-apartheid victims should continue. [5] A different line was taken by Britain's Margaret Thatcher who said "it no longer makes sense to discourage new investment in South Africa". [6] Mandela was hailed for "his indomitable courage". [7] The Commonwealth Secretary General said that South Africa must guarantee Mandela's safety until change there becomes irreversible.
