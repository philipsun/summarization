[1] Iraq's powerful army invaded Kuwait early on 2 August 1990. [2] The small Kuwait army offered little resistance. [3] Kuwaiti government buildings were occupied and 350 Iraqi tanks swept toward the southern oilfields. [4] The invasion followed Hussein's claim that Kuwait stole oil from a disputed border area, and exceeded OPEC production quotas. [5] Kuwait dismissed Iraq's claim that a Kuwaiti revolutionary group invited the invasion. [6] Iraq had signaled the coming of the invasion by moving 100,000 troops to the border, and CIA warned that it might well occur. [7] The United Nations Security Council immediately condemned the invasion, and on 6 August voted 13-0 to impose sweeping trade and military sanctions against Iraq. [8] United States. [9] Japan and the European community had already cut off the importation of Iraqi oil. [10] Oil prices soared. [11] Iraq felt an almost immediate effect, and closed one of the pipelines that carries export oil across Turkey. [12] Iraq retaliated by rounding up westerners in Kuwait, and moving some to Baghdad. [13] President Bush denounced the invasion. [14] A United States aircraft battle group moved into the Arabian Sea. [15] Saddam Hussein warned the Iraqi populace that United States might attack. [16] Bush only said that all options were open.
