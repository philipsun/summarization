[1] In Japan, Emperor Hirohito was simply called Tenno Heika, meaning His Majesty The Emperor. [2] After his death he was posthumously named Showa, the name he chose for his reign soon after he became emperor in 1926. [3] It means Enlightened Peace, and has been succeeded by Heisei, or Achieving Peace, the name the government chose for the reign of Hirohito's son Akihito a few hours after the emperor died on Jan. 7. [4] In Japan, years are numbered according to the emperor's tenure on the throne. [5] Thus, 1926 was Showa 1 and 1989 was Showa 64 until Hirohito's death. [6] Now this year is Heisei 1.
