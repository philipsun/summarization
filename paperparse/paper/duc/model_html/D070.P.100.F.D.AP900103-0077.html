[1] Former East German leader, Erich Honecker, may be moved to a monastery to protect him from a possible lynching by enraged citizens. [2] He is under investigation for corruption and living in luxury at the cost of the state. [3] He was placed under house arrest in December 1989. [4] Both Lutheran and Catholic churches have offered Honecker refuge. [5] It was the churches that became venues for pro-democracy groups that eventually brought the overthrow of Honecker's 18 year leadership. [6] Recently, East German groups have called for punishing Honecker and moving him to smaller quarters. [7] The current leadership fears that in such a situation, Honecker might be attacked.
