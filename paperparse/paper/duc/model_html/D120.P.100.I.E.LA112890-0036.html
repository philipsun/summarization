[1] At 47, John Major, the son of a circus performer and a high school drop out, has become Britain's 18th and youngest prime minister. [2] Major was elected leader of the Conservative Party following the sudden resignation of Margaret Thatcher last week. [3] Thatcher resigned following a leadership challenge by former Defense Minister Michael Heseltine. [4] In the second round balloting following the resignation, Major received 185 votes, two short of the majority needed. [5] His two opponents, Heseltine and Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd, quickly conceded to Major. [6] Major's election is seen as ensuring the unity of the Conservative Party in the next general election.
