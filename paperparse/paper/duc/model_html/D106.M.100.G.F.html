[1] Nelson Mandela, a leader in the African National Congress' opposition to South Africa's policy of apartheid, spent nearly 27 years in prison. [2] He was sentenced to life in prison in 1964 for conspiring to overthrow the government. [3] He became a rallying point for nations that were pressuring South Africa to end its all-white government. [4] World leaders hailed Mandela's release in 1990, and he was welcomed as a hero when he traveled, including a visit to New York City. [5] In-fighting among black elements complicated Mandela's role in negotiations to end white-minority rule in South Africa. [6] Also, Mandela's wife, Winnie, was imprisoned, complicating matters.
