[1] Yesterday the judges announced the short-list of the six fiction writers in contention for the 1992 Booker Prize. [2] This year the prize will be worth 20,000 ponds. [3] The Booker is no longer the richest literary prize, but even making the short-list guarantees the author immense prestige and increased sales for his novel. [4] Critics say the hype afforded Booker finalists does a disservice to other authors who do not make the list. [5] Bookmakers immediately made Michael Ondaatje's "The English Patient" the favorite to win the prize. [6] The winner will be named at Guildhall banquet on 13 October.
