[1] Poland's Solidarity Government's first definitive statement on the issue of a reunified German state said such a state "must be formed gradually and respect Poland's borders". [2] Some right-wing West German politicians have called for recovering the 40,000 square miles of territory that Germany lost to Poland at the end of World War II. [3] The territory is about one-third of present-day Poland. [4] East and West Germany have each separately recognized the present border, but there has been no official assurance that a reunified Germany would. [5] Poland's Foreign Minister Skubiszewski reassured Poles that its government will insist on current borders.
