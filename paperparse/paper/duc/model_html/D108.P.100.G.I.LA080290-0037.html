[1] The dispute between Iraq and Kuwait began when Iraq accused Kuwait of flooding the oil market and stealing $2.4 billion worth of oil. [2] On July 20, Kuwaiti officials responded that Iraq wanted them to forgive war debts. [3] On July 24, the United States announced a "short- notice exercise" in the Persian Gulf and Kuwait went on alert. [4] Saddam Hussein then summoned the U.S. ambassador for talks. [5] OPEC ministers then agreed to halt overproduction and the two nations met to negotiate, although 100,000 Iraqi troops were on the border. [6] Talks collapsed the next day and Iraq invaded at 2 a.m. on August 2.
