[1] Three of President Bush's ambassadorial nominees won narrow approval from the Senate Foreign Relations Committee. [2] Democrats said the only qualifications of the three were political connections. [3] Those nominees were Joseph Zappala for Spain, Marvin Sembler for Australia, and Della Newman for New Zealand. [4] Senator Paul Sarbanes led the opposition in committee and vowed to carry the fight to the Senate floor. [5] The committee routinely approved Shirley Temple Black for Czechoslovakia. [6] Other ambassadorial approvals were Thomas Melady to the Vatican, Keith Brown to Denmark and Joseph Gildenhorn to Switzerland. [7] William Howard Taft IV was approved to be US representative on the council of NATO.
