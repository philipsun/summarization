[1] This morning New York City's Chinatown responded to events in Beijing. [2] More than 5,000 people chanting, "Freedom will survive"! packed a Chinatown park in a memorial service for students killed by soldiers in Tiananmen Square. [3] The crowd stood respectfully and silently during a traditional funeral ceremony and then marched through the streets in a funeral procession waving banners honoring "the martyrs of Tiananmen Square". [4] As they headed up Mulberry Street, a major street in Chinatown, they walked beneath a 30-foot black banner. [5] Demonstrators planned another rally later in the day outside the United Nations.
