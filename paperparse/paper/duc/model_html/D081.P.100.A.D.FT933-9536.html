[1] Coal miners from Romania's Jiu Valley, who spearheaded violent anti-reform rampages through Bucharest in 1990 and 1991, are once again out on strike, pressing the government for higher wages. [2] So far the government, unlike its predecessor has not caved in but has begun action to have the strike declared illegal. [3] Romanians, most of who earn far less than the striking miners, appear solidly opposed to the strike. [4] The miners want more compensation for the difficult conditions in which they work and live. [5] They are also following their charismatic leader, Miron Cosma, who led them on previously successful strikes.
