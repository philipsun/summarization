[1] Rescuers searched through debris today for victims of an earthquake that ravaged one- third of Nepal and parts of India, killing at least 650 people and injuring thousands. [2] Officials in both countries said they had not yet received reports from the remote Himalayan areas hardest hit by the tremors. [3] In eastern Nepal, 50,000 to 60,000 houses were destroyed in the Dharan district alone. [4] Monsoons, earthquake damage and aftershocks have paralyzed train service and road travel hampering relief efforts. [5] In India, more than 25,000 houses were flattened in Darbhanga and Munger, and in the district of Madhubani nearly 50 villages were flooded.
