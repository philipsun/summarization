[1] An overcrowded ferry sank Wednesday night where the Ilisha, Tetulia, and Meghna Rivers meet in southern Bangladesh. [2] It went out of control while turning, in 22 feet of water. [3] The ferry carried double its authorized capacity of 100. [4] People on the roof swam to safety but 100 drowned, including most of those in the hull. [5] Another ferry on the Meghna River, the M.V. Sagar, was attacked by river pirates Tuesday near Chandpur. [6] Fifty pirates boarded as passengers, and shortly before midnight fired rifles and hurled homemade bombs. [7] Two hundred passengers were wounded and six thrown overboard. [8] The pirates took $83,000 and escaped in waiting boats.
