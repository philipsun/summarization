[1] The 25,000-strong Iranian community in San Diego has responded quickly to the earthquake that hit northern Iran on Wednesday, California time. [2] Many have turned anxiety over the fate of relatives and friends into action by mobilizing relief efforts, setting up two offices (467-1120 and 456-4000) to gather contributions for the United Nations Fund for the Iran Earthquake. [3] The Iranian group is collecting medicine, light clothing, blankets, and flashlights among other things. [4] Another group collecting medical supplies is Southwest Medical Teams (284-7979). [5] Donations for Iranian earthquake relief may also be sent to the Red Cross, UNICEF, Bank Meli Iran, and the Adventist Development and Relief Agency.
