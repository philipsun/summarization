[1] After slumbering for 600 years, the Mt. [2] Pinatubo volcano erupted in June 1991. [3] Mt. Pinatubo is on the Philippine Island of Luzon. [4] It first erupted on 9 June, and the eruptions continued through the 14th, with as many as six eruptions on one day. [5] The Philippine Institute of Vulcanology and Seismology, and the United States Geological Survey said the volcanic activity was increasing in a way that suggested a more massive eruption would occur. [6] Heavy rain increased the danger of mudslides. [7] The mountain shot out gases, rock and ash. [8] Mushroom clouds ascended to heights of ten miles. [9] The gray-green clouds turned day into night, and covered wide areas with ash. [10] More than 19,000 panicked villagers within a 12-mile radius were evacuated. [11] Clark Air Base, one of the largest U.S. overseas installations, is about ten miles from Pinatubo. [12] Most airmen, dependents and civilian employees were evacuated to the U.S. [13] Naval Base at Subic Bay on the 11th, following the first major eruption. [14] The remaining personnel left a day or two later. [15] Philippine President Aquino dismissed as a fabrication that she had been warned volcanic damage could cause a nuclear incident.
