[1] Australian novelist Peter Carey was awarded the Booker Prize for fiction Tuesday for his mid- 19th century Australian love story, "Oscar and Lucinda". [2] A panel of five judges, who unanimously announced the award of the $26,250 prize, made their selection from 102 books published in Britain in the last 12 months and which they read in their homes. [3] Carey was the only non-Briton among the exceptionally strong competitors in the final six. [4] Book Trust, an educational charity promoting books, administers the Booker Prize that was first awarded in 1969. [5] It is sponsored by Booker, an international food and farming business.
