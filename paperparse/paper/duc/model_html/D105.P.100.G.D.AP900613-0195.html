[1] Ethnic violence killed 148 people and wounded 885 in the Central Republic of Kirghizia in the Soviet Union. [2] The clashes arose over a land dispute between ethnic Kirghiz and Uzbeks, both Sunni Moslems. [3] In two years of civil strife, mainly ethnic clashes, 24,000 have been arrested. [4] Troops had to be used in 13 regions of the Soviet Unions to deal with the unrest. [5] Besides unrest in Kirghizia and Uzbekistan, ethnic violence has also been reported in Georgia, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan and Tadzhikistan. [6] These conflicts were often accompanied by riots, arson and the massacre of innocent people.
