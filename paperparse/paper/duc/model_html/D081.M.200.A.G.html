[1] Worldwide industrial strikes for wage increases 1987-1994: [2] August 4, 1987: 200,000 South African black gold and coals miners may begin a strike. [3] August 17-18, 1988: Coalmine workers in Jastrzebie, southwest of Warsaw, went on strike to demand legalization of Solidarity and higher wages. [4] Szczecin port workers and Morcinek mine workers declared sympathy strikes. [5] June 16, 1989: Pennsylvania, Indiana and Kentucky coal miners walked out in sympathy for miners in Virginia and West Virginia. [6] July 19-21, 1989: Strikes in the Soviet Union by coal miners and factory workers have shut down over 200 mines in the country's top coal-producing regions from the Ukraine through Siberia and Central Asia. [7] November 2, 1989: Miners in the Northern Vorkuta region expanded their strike and blocked coal shipments. [8] June 6, 1991: A one-day coal miners' strike in Kazakhstan and the Ukraine spread to all major coal regions. [9] July 21, 1992: A copper-producing combine in Lublin, Poland went on strike as coal-mining unions staged stoppages in a third of the country's pits. [10] August 9, 1993: Coal miners in Romania's Jui Valley went on strike again. [11] June 29, 1994: Miners at six coal production associations and pits in the Donetsk Region of the Ukraine went on strike.
