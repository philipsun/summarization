[1] A powerful cold front moved through the eastern half of the United States on November 15 and 16, spawning tornados, violent thunderstorms, and heavy rainfall. [2] Thirteen states reported deaths, injuries, or damage. [3] Hardest hit was Huntsville, Alabama, where a tornado leveled a shopping district, apartment development, and elementary school. [4] Seventeen were killed, 463 injured, and 1000 left homeless. [5] Damages were estimated at $100 million. [6] President Bush declared Huntsville a disaster area. [7] Marilyn Quayle and Alabama's governor and senators toured the area on November 18. [8] This storm front killed nine others, including seven school children in Newburgh, New York.
