[1] In an effort to raise funds, Maxwell Communications Corporation made late payments on the foreign market. [2] Called teaming and lading, Maxwell and his son Kevin began to buy and sell enormous amounts of foreign currencies. [3] Maxwell would buy currency in one financial center and promise to repay it in another. [4] MCC received the currency, but on a number of occasions, did not make the payment, giving itself an unauthorized loan. [5] About 20 banks, including Lloyds, Bank of America, Goldman Sachs, and Swiss Bank Corporation were involved in the exchange deals. [6] At least 100m dollars in credit is thought to have been raised this way.
