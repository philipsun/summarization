[1] A row has erupted between the Bombay stock exchange and the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) over its first inspection of the stockbrokers' books. [2] SEBI accused the brokers of ignoring rules and authorities of failing to enforce rules and of poor administration. [3] The exchange denied the charges and called SEBI heavy handed and inflexible. [4] The stock market has fallen 48 percent since early last year including dropping 320 points after the Indian budget was announced. [5] Foreign investors are concerned. [6] This is just the latest in a series of disputes between SEBI--backed by the finance minister--and brokers.
