[1] Friday, President Corazon Aquino refused to allow Ferdinand Marcos to return to the Philippines to die or permit his body to be buried here. [2] Marcos is in critical condition in Honolulu. [3] His wife, Imelda, broadcast an appeal to allow his return and said she was trying to contact the president. [4] The president's daughter denied receiving any calls. [5] Aquino, whose government has survived six coup attempts, cited security reasons, but said she was praying for Marcos' recovery. [6] Marcos' supporters are planning a rally for Saturday and warned of demonstrations if he should die in exile. [7] The Philippine military said law and order will prevail.
