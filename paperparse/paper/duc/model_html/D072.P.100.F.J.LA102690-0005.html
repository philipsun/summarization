[1] I remember the Rennaissance man of music, Leonard Bernstein, from October 1948. [2] I was an American volunteer in the Israeli army. [3] Once, courtesy of the Israeli equivalent to the USO, I attended a performance of the Israel Philharmonic when Bernstein was guest conductor. [4] Black-tie patrons and soldiers in fatigues comprised the audience. [5] The program included Beethoven's emotional Fifth Symphony. [6] Intermittently for most of the symphony, we could hear machine-gun fire from the Jordanian Legion held sector. [7] The crackling sound melded with the music and seemed entirely appropriate to the mood and circumstances. [8] Lenny and the orchestra never missed a beat.
