[1] Investigators say the late publishing magnate Robert Maxwell siphoned money from his companies in an attempt to deal with crushing debts. [2] A government prosecution agency investigating his Mirror Group pension fund assets says that $630 million was taken from the fund without authority. [3] His son Kevin says the debts are so enormous nothing is left for the pensioners. [4] Reportedly, Maxwell never created the pension fund promised to New York Daily News managers. [5] Creditors face losses of $905 million. [6] Kevin admitted his father was a crook, and he and his brother Ian are under investigation for their knowledge of Robert Maxwell's companies.
