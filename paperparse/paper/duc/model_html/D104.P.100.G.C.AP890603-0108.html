[1] The confrontation between Chinese troops and hundreds of thousands of demonstrators ended in the killing and wounding of untold numbers in Tiananmen Square. [2] The protesters, mainly students along with some workers and intellectuals, finally were forced to leave Tiananmen early in the morning of 4 June. [3] The bloody encounter was the climax of trouble that had been escalating since April. [4] As early as 27 April more than 150,000 surged past police lines and filled Tiananmen Square. [5] Other demonstrations followed, and the government imposed martial law on 20 May. [6] The students defied the government's order to leave, and blocked streets leading to the square.
