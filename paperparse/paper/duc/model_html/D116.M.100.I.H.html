[1] On April 24, 1990, shuttle Discovery carried the 12.5-ton Hubble Space Telescope 380 miles into space. [2] Orbiting above Earth's distorting atmosphere, Hubble would see 10 times better than the largest Earth telescopes. [3] Technical problems exacerbated by conservative system settings put Hubble several days behind schedule. [4] Discovery's five astronauts were all veterans of previous flights. [5] Discovery followed Hubble for two days to assist if necessary. [6] Spacelab Astro was to be put up two weeks later to give additional target information. [7] Hubble cost $1.5 billion, with millions more projected for support over its 15-year lifetime. [8] Discovery carried a telescope eyepiece used by Edwin Hubble, for whom the Hubble is named.
