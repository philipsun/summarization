[1] More than 100 people were feared drowned after an overcrowded ferry sank at a river junction in southern Bangladesh. [2] It was carrying more than double its authorized capacity of 100 passengers when it sank in 22 feet of water. [3] Those on the ferry's roof swam to safety, but most of those in the hull drowned. [4] River pirates attacked another ferry, throwing six people overboard. [5] The pirates, who had boarded the ferry as passengers, open fire and exploded bombs as people were bedding down for the night. [6] The attackers, who made off with about 83,000 in local currency, escaped in waiting boats.
