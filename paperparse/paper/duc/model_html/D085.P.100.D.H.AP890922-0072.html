[1] At the National Hurricane Center in Coral Gables, Florida, forecasters working 12-hour days monitored Hurricane Hugo using computers and satellites. [2] They calculated where it would strike and got emergency operations going in those areas. [3] Director Bob Sheets appeared on TV all week and credited the ever-present but bothersome media with getting the word out. [4] A hurricane warning, involving emergency preparations and evacuation plans, costs $50 million. [5] Before the warning system was established, a Hugo- sized storm would have killed thousands. [6] So far there were no reports of coastal-area deaths. [7] After Hugo's landfall, forecasters relaxed but still followed three other Atlantic weather systems and one in the Pacific.
