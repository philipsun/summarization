[1] Hurricane Hugo did not simply wreak devastation on the Carolinas; it brought about repayment of a long overdue debt. [2] Kingston, N.Y., the capital of New York in 1777, was burned by the British in October of that year. [3] New York Gov. George Clinton received from South Carolina Gov. Abraham Livingston a letter dated March 31, 1778 enclosing 1,000 New York pounds "...for the charitable purpose of alleviating the distresses of the now-indigent inhabitants of the town of Kingston...". [4] A local historian remembered the letter and now Kingston is raising money to help Charleston, S.C. recover from the ravages of Hugo.
