[1] In the aftermath of the coup, Gorbachev fired his foreign minister and acting defense minister. [2] Boris Yeltsin fired the heads of the state-owned media. [3] The Moscow City Council ordered all Communist Party buildings in the capital sealed and telephone service cut off to party and KGB buildings. [4] Speaking before the Russian Parliament, Gorbachev affirmed Yeltsin's decrees issued during the coup as legitimate. [5] Yeltsin commented several times during Gorbachev's speech and Gorbachev deferred to him several times for details of happenings during his detention. [6] Six of the top party officials were arrested for instigating the coup.
