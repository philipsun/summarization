[1] Port workers in Szczecin, Poland and southwestern coal miners declared sympathy strikes with miners demanding the legalization of Solidarity according to opposition activists. [2] A local prosecutor declared the strike illegal. [3] In support of 3000 colleagues striking for a second day at a mine in Jastrzebie, workers at a nearby mine refused to take up their tools and launched their own strike. [4] The strikes mark the eighth anniversary of the birth of Solidarity, which Communist authorities crushed in a 1981 military crackdown. [5] Miners are demanding the legalization of Solidarity, pay raises and reinstatement of workers fired for union activities.
