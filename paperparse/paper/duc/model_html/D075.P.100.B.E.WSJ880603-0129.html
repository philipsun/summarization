[1] Doctors are being cautioned not to routinely prescribe aspirin in the prevention of heart attacks. [2] This word from the Food and Drug Administration comes after findings which suggest that aspirin therapy may increase the possibility of stroke in some patients. [3] Individuals most at risk are those with blood clotting problems or high blood pressure. [4] This warning comes after a study, published in January, which indicated that an aspirin taken every other day reduced the risk of a non-fatal first heart attack by 47 percent as compared to a group taking a non-aspirin substitute.
