[1] By a 13-0 vote (Cuba and Yemen abstaining), the UN approved trade and military sanctions against Iraq, including a ban on oil purchases, as punishment for invading Kuwait. [2] Iraq reacted by rounding up Britons, Americans and West Germans in Kuwait, taking some to Iraq. [3] Saddam Hussein ordered evacuation drills in Baghdad in case of US or Israeli attacks. [4] Many Kuwaitis fled to Saudi Arabia. [5] Oil prices soared to a four-year high. [6] One reason for invading Kuwait was Kuwaiti reluctance to slow its oil flow to create higher prices, which Iraq was pressing for to rebuild its war-damaged economy and settle $80 billion in foreign debts.
