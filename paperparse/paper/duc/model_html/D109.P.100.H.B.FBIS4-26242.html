[1] Floods have hit Changsha, the capital of central China's Hunan Province following a succession of rainstorms in the upper and middle Xiangjiang River. [2] Late this afternoon the water level at the Changsha monitoring station had risen to 38.93 meters, some 0.56 meters higher than ever previously recorded. [3] The water level is still rising sharply. [4] Yesterday afternoon 19 dykes in the Changsha area collapsed affecting 128,000 residents. [5] The Changsha city government has asked those in the endangered area to move to safer places. [6] So far, more than 500,000 soldiers and local residents are fighting the floods. [7] Communist Party officials urge perseverance.
