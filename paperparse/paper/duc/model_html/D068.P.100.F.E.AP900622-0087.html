[1] Soviet Foreign Minister Eduard Schevardnadze outlined the Soviet proposal allowing a united Germany to join NATO after a five-year transition period. [2] The proposal came during international talks in East Berlin on the strategic future of a united Germany. [3] The U.S. and its NATO allies object to the proposal. [4] The proposal also called for the departure of Western troops from West Berlin within six months as well as a 50 percent reduction of the troops of all fours nations in the country within five years, eventually reducing them further to a level of token contingents or completely withdrawing from Germany.
