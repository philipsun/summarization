[1] The Senate panel considering John Tower's nomination as Secretary of Defense has found discrepancies in his dealings with defense contractors. [2] The panel members cited differences in his testimony before the group and what he wrote in a deposition given at the time of his divorce from his second wife in 1987. [3] Tower had told the panel that he only represented British Aerospace, Inc. on matters of civil aviation. [4] In the deposition, however, he wrote that he had given the company advice on selling certain systems to the defense department. [5] The panel also is concerned about Tower's drinking habits and reports of womanizing.
