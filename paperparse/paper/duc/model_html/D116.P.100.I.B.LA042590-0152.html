[1] The shuttle Discovery astronauts triumphantly launched the $1.5 billion Hubble Space Telescope. [2] It happened one orbit late, after last-ditch commands from Earth freed a stuck solar panel, averting an emergency spacewalk by crewmembers standing by to pull out the array if necessary. [3] The telescope's batteries were only good for eight hours. [4] With the troublesome panel unfurled, astronaut Steve Crawley used the shuttle's mechanical arm to lift the 12.5 ton Hubble from the cargo bay. [5] Discovery then backed away as the telescope, orbiting Earth every 97.3 minutes at an altitude of 380 miles, began its 15-year search for new worlds.
