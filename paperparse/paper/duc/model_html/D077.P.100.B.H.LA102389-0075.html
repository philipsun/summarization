[1] Charleston and San Francisco are united by suffering two of the century's costliest, back- to-back natural disasters, Hurricane Hugo and the Bay Area earthquake. [2] Both endured disruption of normal life, destroyed buildings, billions in damages, loss of life, and frustrating delays in getting federal assistance. [3] The two disasters were different because Hugo gave Charleston warning. [4] Its damage was everywhere, over quickly, and immediately evident, whereas earthquakes leave hidden damage and have aftershocks. [5] Charleston recovered slowly and disrupted communications left it isolated. [6] San Francisco's earthquake was reported immediately and the city bounced back quickly. [7] Charleston lost no major lifeline like the Bay Bridge.
