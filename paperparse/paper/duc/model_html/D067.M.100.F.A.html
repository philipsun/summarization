[1] In August 1992 Hurricane Andrew hit the Bahamas, southern Florida, and Louisiana leaving 19 dead and extensive property damage. [2] In Florida a quarter of a million were left homeless. [3] The cost to insurers, concentrated in U.S. companies, was estimated as high as $20 billion and much of the damaged property was not insured. [4] Federal authorities promised emergency aid after Florida officials complained of delays in supplying food and water. [5] The cost of rebuilding Florida and caring for those displaced by the storm was estimated at $20-$30 billion. [6] Fortunately the storm did not hit low-lying New Orleans or oil facilities along the gulf coast.
