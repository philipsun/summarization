[1] Viktor Alksnis, born in Latvia in 1950 and former deputy of the Supreme Soviet of the former USSR is warning of possible ethnic cleansing in Latvia. [2] He says that the adoption of the Latvian citizenship law violates not only human rights but creates the conditions for a conflict similar to the ones in Yugoslavia and the Caucasus. [3] This law allows for a "mild" ethnic cleansing of the Russian-speaking population of Latvia. [4] According to Alksnis, some circles in Moscow want conflict in Latvia and will use the "mild" Russian cleansing as an excuse for stringent ethnic cleansing of the Latvians.
