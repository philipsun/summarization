[1] An outpouring of sympathy from presidents to childhood neighbors followed the death of Lucille Ball but many said she would live on in reruns and through her influence on comedy. [2] She died at the age of 77, at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center following emergency heart surgery. [3] Miss Ball and her late former husband, Desi Arnaz, starred in "I Love Lucy", from 1951 to 1957. [4] The groundbreaking show, still seen in syndication worldwide, was one of the most popular in television. [5] Her admirers included President Bush and former President Reagan as well as many famous comedians. [6] She was also remembered in her hometown of Jamestown, New York.
