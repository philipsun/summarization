[1] Prime Minister John Major won the general election a year ago, but today he is a deeply unpopular, unproven leader. [2] His party is internally torn over the controversy that accompanied Britain's withdrawal of sterling from the European Monetary System and by a lingering deep recession that has alienated many traditional supporters in business. [3] Most industrialists are grateful that he won the election and he is appreciated for personal qualities that are favorably compared to those of his predecessor. [4] Unlike the former prime minister, however, he is viewed as a weak leader, with little vision and no clearly articulated political conviction.
