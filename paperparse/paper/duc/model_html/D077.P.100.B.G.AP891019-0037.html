[1] Private groups and officials from around the world and the US offered earthquake relief to the San Francisco Bay area. [2] Los Angles shipped doctors, sonic equipment, and fire investigators; Charleston, SC and a California brewery sent drinking water; the Salvation Army provided cooked food canteens; the Red Cross blood units and shelters; and the Coast Guard boats and helicopters. [3] Britain flew in engineers. [4] Israel and France offered search teams, and Osaka, Japan sent $10,000 in cash. [5] President Bush directed $273 million in aid but federal and private coffers, especially the Red Cross, are strained by previous responses to Hurricane Hugo.
