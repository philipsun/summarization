[1] Damage from Hurricane Andrew could rise to $20 billion, making it one of the costliest this century. [2] It is expected to make landfall soon and officials in Mississippi, Louisiana, and Texas have ordered two million people to evacuate and many refineries are closed. [3] Fifteen people have been killed and damage in Florida is estimated to be $15 to $20 billion. [4] Homestead and the nearby air force base were virtually flattened. [5] The U.S. insurance industry will take the brunt of the losses. [6] Primary insurers include the State Farm Group, Allstate, Hartford Insurance, Aetna, and Travellers. [7] The last serious hurricane caused $4.2 billion in insured losses in 1989.
