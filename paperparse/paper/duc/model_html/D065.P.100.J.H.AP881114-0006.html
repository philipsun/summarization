[1] Vice-presidential candidate Dan Quayle, 41, was seen as a political novice and relegated to small towns and safe GOP areas. [2] After controversies subsided he freed himself from handlers and became more accessible but soon dropped off front pages. [3] He is loyal to Bush and willing to be a team player but has few administration intimates. [4] He is criticized as bringing no reputation or staff to the campaign, being untested, and being unqualified to assume the presidency. [5] He is described as immature but not stupid, thrust into a situation for which he was unprepared. [6] Quayle as VP choice is compared to Nixon, chosen to appease conservatives, and political nonentity Spiro Agnew.
