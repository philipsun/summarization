[1] An earthquake measuring 6.9 on the Richter scale rocked Northern California at 5:04pm on October 17, 1989. [2] Felt 225 miles away, it lasted 15 seconds, caused $2 billion damage, and killed 60. [3] Candlestick Park shook and World Series Game Three was postponed. [4] Buildings collapsed because of poor soil and structural design. [5] Chinatown on firm hills and Telegraph, Nob, and Russian Hills on rock escaped major damage. [6] The Marina District, on landfill dredged from the bay, lost 60 buildings and was swept by fire. [7] Apartment buildings with garages across the bottom were most vulnerable. [8] High-rises built to strict earthquake codes swayed with little damage. [9] Part of the Bay Bridge's upper deck collapsed. [10] The 1906 San Francisco quake, estimated at 8.3, killed 2500. [11] Debate waged over whether northern or southern California was due for the next big quake. [12] A 1988 report targeted Tuesday's epicenter, 75 miles south in the Santa Cruz area, as Northern California's most likely spot, with 30% probability of a 6.5 quake within 30 years. [13] Los Angeles, Los Alamos, Israel, and France offered survivor-search help. [14] British engineers assessed structural damage. [15] Other help came from relief organizations, companies, cities, and the federal government.
