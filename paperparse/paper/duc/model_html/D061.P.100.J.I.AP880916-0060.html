[1] Hurricane Gilbert's growth baffles forecasters. [2] On 3 September it was a dry low-pressure trough--one of 50-60 a year--west of Africa. [3] By Tuesday night it was an intense storm with the lowest barometric pressure--26.13 inches--ever recorded. [4] Why did Gilbert strengthened and others not? [5] Scientists do know that budding storms need to be off the equator and are fueled by winds converging where there is high moisture in the lower atmosphere. [6] Clouds may form and the air mass starts spinning, moving heat to the upper atmosphere. [7] The warm September waters in the Caribbean can cause the storm to explode.
