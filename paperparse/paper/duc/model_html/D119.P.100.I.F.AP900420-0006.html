[1] President Bush is expected to name Melissa Wells, currently ambassador to Mozambique, to be ambassador to Nicaragua. [2] The administration apparently believes that a female ambassador can relate better to the new president Violeta Chamorro. [3] Several male career diplomats with experience in Latin American affairs were passed over in the selection of Ms Wells. [4] Ms Wells was born in Estonia and has no Latin American experience but does speak Spanish. [5] The US and Nicaragua have not had ambassadors in each other's country since July 1988 when President Daniel Ortega expelled the US ambassador and President Reagan expelled the Nicaraguan ambassador.
