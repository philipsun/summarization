[1] Critic and novelist Antonia Byatt won the Booker Prize, Britain's most prestigious literary award for "Possession", one of the year's six finalists in the 21-year old competition. [2] She received $39,000 and earlier this month received the $43,000 Times-Aer Lingus prize for international faction for the same work. [3] The Booker Prize was announced at a banquet in London's Elizabethan Guildhall, seat of the city's Lord Mayor. [4] Her book is a tale of two young scholars investigating the lives of a pair of imaginary Victorian poets by retracing the poets' journeys across England and to France. [5] She has published five novels since 1964.
