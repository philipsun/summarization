[1] More than 4,000 workers at a coal mine in Jastrzebie, Poland went on strike today to demand legalization of Solidarity, higher wages and the reinstatement of workers fired for union activities. [2] Workers at a copper mine had staged a protest rally earlier. [3] The strike comes on the eighth anniversary of the founding of the outlawed trade federation. [4] Solidarity was crushed in 1981, when the government imposed martial law and was banned nearly a year later by parliament. [5] In 1980 workers at factories around the port of Gdansk joined striking shipyard workers to form Solidarity, the first and only independent trade federation in the Soviet bloc.
