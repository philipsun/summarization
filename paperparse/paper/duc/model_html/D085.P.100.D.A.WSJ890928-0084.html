[1] Damage from Hurricane Hugo which tore through the Caribbean last week will reduce third-quarter earnings for several insurers. [2] American International Group expects a reduction in pretax earnings of $35-45 million. [3] St. Paul Cos. said the pretax cost shouldn't exceed $50 million and for Cigna Corp. a reduction of 35- 40% in per share earnings on an after-tax basis is predicted. [4] State Farm's State Fire and Casualty Co. estimates payouts of over $500 million, three times the previous record of $165 million set by Hurricane Alicia in 1983. [5] Most of these estimates take into account recoveries from the companies' reinsurance contracts.
