[1] Planes loaded with relief supplies for Iran's earthquake victims arrived at Tehran airport from around the world. [2] One of the planes carried a private US relief mission, the first in more than a decade. [3] Delivery of the supplies from Tehran to the stricken north was hampered by aftershocks that caused landslides across some of the roads. [4] Dozens of nations sent people and all sorts of supplies to the devastated country. [5] US aid came from AmeriCares, a private relief organization, and more would come via a Red Cross charter flight carrying $225,000 worth of supplies donated by the US government.
