[1] A ranking member of Chairman's Helmut Kohl's party demanded that the Soviet Union turn over Erich Honecker, who is wanted by German authorities on manslaughter charges. [2] A Berlin court has issued an arrest warrant for Honecker, 78, for issuing shoot- to-kill orders to East German guards at the now-fallen Berlin Wall. [3] Honecker has denied all charges against him, including that he was responsible for the estimated 200 people who died trying to escape East Germany. [4] Honecker's fate is one of the first issues facing Bonn and Moscow since they signed several treaties designed to draw the two nations closer.
