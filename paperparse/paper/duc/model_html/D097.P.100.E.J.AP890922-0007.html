[1] Hugo, a category 4 hurricane, struck South Carolina Thursday after thousands of coastal residents in southeastern states fled inland. [2] A Red Cross spokesman said that it was sheltering thousands of evacuees and resources were very strained. [3] A National Hurricane Center official said hurricane-force winds first hit the coast by 9:15 p.m. EDT. [4] About three hours later the eye of the storm, 10 miles across and with top winds at 135 mph, passed over the Isle of Palms east of Charleston. [5] By midnight, 119 mph winds were reported in Charleston. [6] The roof of City Hall, a 188- year-old brick building, cracked.
