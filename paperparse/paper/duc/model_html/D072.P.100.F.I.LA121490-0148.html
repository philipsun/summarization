[1] The Broadway theatrical community said goodbye Thursday to Leonard Bernstein at the Majestic Theater. [2] The public started lining up at 7 a.m. to join invited friends, relatives and theatrical personalities. [3] Arthur Laurents, the librettist for "West Side Story", was host. [4] Other collaborators, original cast members, and old friends, several coming from California, spoke and performed. [5] His son, Alexander, delivered an affectionate talk, while Michael Wager, a very close friend at the end, spoke movingly about Bernstein's final illness. [6] The program closed with Marilyn Horne singing "Somewhere", the emotional anthem from "West Side Story". [7] A sustained standing ovation ended the evening.
