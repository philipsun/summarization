[1] Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev returned to power as the coup against him collapsed, unraveling swiftly as junta was unable to cope with thousands of people who created barricades around the Russian Parliament, Russian President Yeltsin's stronghold. [2] The national legislature demanded Gorbachev's reinstatement and the Communist Party, seeking to salvage its image, denounced the takeover. [3] The real issue for the coup failure was not Gorbachev but the new political culture of democracy he initiated. [4] At first Gorbachev and Yeltsin appeared as equals but Yeltsin, who challenged the Communist Party and stayed close to the Russian people, trumped Gorbachev.
