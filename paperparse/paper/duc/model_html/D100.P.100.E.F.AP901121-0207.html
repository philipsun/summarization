[1] John Lennon had been in virtual seclusion for five years, taking care of his son, Sean, and, admittedly out of songwriting ideas. [2] But, in 1980, he again felt inspired and returned to the studio to make an album. [3] Musicians who worked with Lennon on what were to be his final two albums considered him to be a hero. [4] Songs that the former Beatle wrote for these albums were mostly mainstream pop; no political anthems. [5] He was a happy man; his singing was higher and sweeter than before. [6] He wrote songs that were tributes to his wife, Yoko Ono, and to Sean.
