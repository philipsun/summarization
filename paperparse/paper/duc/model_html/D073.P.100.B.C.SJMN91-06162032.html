[1] Mount Pinatubo erupted Sunday, the first eruption in over 600 years, shooting out gases, rock and ash. [2] Eruptions continued today, leaving the mountain enshrouded in thick gray clouds that rose to 12,000 feet. [3] It had recently shown signs of activity, and Philippine scientists now say a catastrophe is possible. [4] Rain last night raised the additional fear of mudslides. [5] Clark Air Force Base, one of the largest U.S. installations overseas, is ten miles from the mountain. [6] Most airmen, dependents and civilian employees were evacuated to the U.S. [7] Naval Base at Subic Bay, 50 miles from Clark.
