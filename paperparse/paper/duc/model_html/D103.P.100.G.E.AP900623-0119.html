[1] Supplies, medical personnel, and condolences have begun to arrive in Iran where about 40,000 died in an earthquake. [2] Responding to the humanitarian need, countries both friendly and hostile to Tehran's Islamic government have contributed to the relief effort. [3] The United States, which broke diplomatic relations with Iran, will provide aid through the American Red Cross. [4] In addition to supplies, the Soviet Union sent a professional life saving team from the Armenian capital of Yerevan. [5] The French have said they will send a mobile hospital unit if Iran wants it. [6] Pope John Paul II also donated to the effort.
