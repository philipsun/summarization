[1] Mikhail Gorbachev and Boris Yeltsin, appearing to act as equals, launched a sweeping purge of the Communist Party today. [2] Gorbachev fired his foreign minister and named a new head of the KGB. [3] Yeltsin fired the heads of state-owned media. [4] All of Moscow's Communist Party buildings were sealed as protesters blockaded the Party headquarters to prevent removal of document linking Party members with the coup. [5] Gorbachev affirmed Yeltsin's decrees to ban party activities in the Russian republic, the police and KGB, but balked at proposals to ban the Communist Party. [6] It wasn't clear whether Gorbachev had intended to back Yeltsin's decrees.
