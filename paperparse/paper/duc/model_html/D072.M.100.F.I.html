[1] America's maestro, Leonard Bernstein died at 72 on October 14. [2] Suffering from lung diseases since spring, he announced his retirement four days earlier. [3] "Lenny" was the first American conductor to attain world fame. [4] He composed both classical and popular music and introduced thousands to classical music on television. [5] He made more than 200 classical recordings, many now being examined for re-release. [6] Posthumous plaudits have come from everywhere, with memorial classical concerts in New York and Los Angles, as well as a goodbye from Broadway. [7] His 70th birthday celebrations had featured all-star fundraising concerts at the Tanglewood Music Center and Carnegie Hall.
