[1] Emperor Hirohito of Japan died in 1989 at the age of 87. [2] He ascended to the throne in 1926 and presided over one of Japan's most tumultuous eras. [3] The last of the World War II generation of leaders, Hirohito was a controversial figure, even in death. [4] While many western leaders praised him, many Asian leaders, whose countries suffered under the Japanese military during World War II were cool. [5] While most historians believe that Hirohito was a powerless figurehead, others believe that he had to have known of the massacre of thousands of Chinese in Nanking and of the plan to bomb Pearl Harbor. [6] Some believe that the U.S. covered up his complicity in war crimes after the war to avoid unrest in Japan. [7] After the U.S. dropped the atomic bomb on Japan, Hirohito announced Japan's surrender in a radio address to his nation. [8] After the war, Hirohito was just a symbol but the imperial system has remained important to the Japanese people. [9] Hirohito had traveled in Europe and had a preference for Western food and clothes. [10] With few real responsibilities, he spent much time in the study of marine biology. [11] He died of cancer after a long illness and is succeeded by his son Akihito.
