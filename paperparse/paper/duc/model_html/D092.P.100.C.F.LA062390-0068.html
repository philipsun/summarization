[1] The US government has joined with private humanitarian groups to send aid to earthquake-ravaged Iran. [2] The government gave $300,000 of supplies, including hard hats, leather gloves, face masks, wool blankets, and tents. [3] Delivery of the goods would probably be by private charters rather than military transports which would normally do the job. [4] Death count from the earthquake stands at 29,000 and about 400,000 are homeless. [5] Charitable organizations across are collecting money for Iranian relief. [6] The American Jewish World Service is in a quandary wondering if they should provide assistance since Iran has excluded Israel as a source of aid.
