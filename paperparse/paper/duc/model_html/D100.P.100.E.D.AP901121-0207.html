[1] In the summer of 1980, drummer Andy Newmark was asked to play in John Lennon's new album. [2] Lennon had been in seclusion for five years, but in 1980 felt inspired to make an album with his wife Yoko Ono. [3] Newmark spent a month in the studio playing the songs that eventually ended up on "Double Fantasy" and "Milk and Honey", Lennon's final albums. [4] It was Newmark said, the highlight of my career. [5] Prior to meeting Lennon, Newmark was nervous about working with him, but found that Lennon was just a "working musician". [6] It took Newmark a long time to recover from Lennon's death.
