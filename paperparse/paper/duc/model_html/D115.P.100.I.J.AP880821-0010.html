[1] An earthquake measuring 6.5 on the Richter scale struck the India-Nepal border region Sunday, triggering landslides and destroying houses. [2] In India, least 100 people were killed in Bihar and at least 1000 more injured in house collapses in Bihar and surrounding states. [3] In Nepal, authorities said at least 137 were killed and more than 500 were injured. [4] Officials of both countries expect the death toll to rise after they receive reports from the harder hit remote areas. [5] Both countries were sending emergency medical teams to the area. [6] Rescue operations are being hampered by the monsoon rains which began in late June.
