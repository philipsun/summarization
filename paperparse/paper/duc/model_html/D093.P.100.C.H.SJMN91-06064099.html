[1] A ferry carrying 600 Somalis struck a reef and sank Friday off Kenya, near the tourist resort of Malindi. [2] One hundred thirty bodies were recovered but 50 more were believed still trapped. [3] Most who drowned were on the lower deck. [4] First word of the accident came when survivors staggered into a hotel, which sent rescue boats. [5] The ferry was one of a stream of vessels bringing Somali refugees to Kenya after rebels overthrew Somali President Siad Barre in late January. [6] The month-long fighting had largely stopped but refugees continued to flee to Kenya. [7] Eleven Somalis drowned when a boat capsized north of Malindi on February 1st.
