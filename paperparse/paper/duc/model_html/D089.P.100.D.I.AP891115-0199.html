[1] Storms roared across the middle Mississippi Valley and the Southeast on Wednesday, bringing tornadoes, high winds, and baseball-sized hail. [2] These storms were created as a strong cold front moved eastward from Missouri and Arkansas. [3] Tornados pounded Huntsville, Alabama. [4] Tornados also were identified or suspected in southern Illinois, northern Georgia, South Carolina, and Tennessee. [5] Severe thunderstorms hit in Arkansas, Ohio, and Kentucky. [6] Peru, Indiana reported the heaviest rainfall. [7] Other storms, with locally heavy rain, were scattered along the northern Atlantic Coast, which had been experiencing unseasonably warm weather. [8] Snow continued in the West and upper Midwest.
