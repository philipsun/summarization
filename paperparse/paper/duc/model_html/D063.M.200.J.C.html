[1] An explosion at 8:28 A.M. on 22 September destroyed a military barracks in Deal, a town southeast of London in County Kent. [2] The barracks was home to the Royal Marines School of Music. [3] Ten military bandsmen were killed, and as many as 22 were injured, eight seriously. [4] Neighbors expressed shock at the strength of the blast. [5] No serious injuries to civilians were reported, but neighboring homes suffered heavy damage. [6] The IRA claimed responsibility for the attack, linking it what they called a warlike speech delivered by Mrs. Thatcher during a recent visit to Northern Ireland. [7] Investigators said the damage was illustrative of that caused by a bomb. [8] The IRA has a record of attacking military installations, and this latest attack was the more damaging than any in more than seven years. [9] The Prime Ministers of Britain and Ireland have denounced the attack. [10] Police are seeking three men with Irish accents who lived near the barracks for a short time. [11] Private security firms serve the barracks at Deal and 29 other bases in Britain. [12] Families of the victims and opposition party leaders have criticized the arrangement, blaming the attack on lax security because of the private guards.
