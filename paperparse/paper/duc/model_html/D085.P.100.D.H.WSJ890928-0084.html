[1] Damage from Hurricane Hugo, which stormed through the Carolinas and Caribbean last week, will reduce several insurers' third-quarter earnings. [2] American International Group, which insured property in Puerto Rico, the eastern Caribbean, and the Carolina coast, expects losses between $35 and $45 million. [3] St. Paul Cos. said the pretax cost of Hugo shouldn't exceed $50 million. [4] Cigna Corp.'s third-quarter per-share earnings could drop 50-65 cents a share. [5] State Farm's Fire & Casualty unit estimates $400 million in payments in South Carolina. [6] Payments in North Carolina and Virginia will bring the total to $500 million, more than three times its 1983 record payout of $165 million for Hurricane Alicia.
