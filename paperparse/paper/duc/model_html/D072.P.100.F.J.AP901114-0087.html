[1] Exactly 47 years after Leonard Bernstein debuted as conductor of New York Philharmonic, the orchestra remembered the maestro at a concert that included selections from his "Mass," "Chichester Psalms," and "Candide". [2] Mr. Bernstein died on October 16th. [3] Family members and friends of the world renown conductor introduced various selections. [4] An international roster of musicians and singers joined the orchestra as guest artists. [5] Conductors were Michael Tillson Thomas, Michael Barrett, Christoph Eschenbach and James Levine. [6] The orchestra played the overture to "Candide" with a symbolically empty podium, which brought tears to some of the invited audience and a standing ovation.
