[1] A violent earthquake registering 6.9 on the Richter scale rocked Northern California on Tuesday. [2] One report said the quake was centered in the Santa Cruz area 75 miles south of San Francisco. [3] Initial reports indicated widespread damage, especially affecting highways and old masonry buildings. [4] A section of both The San Francisco Bridge and Oakland-San Francisco Bridge collapsed. [5] World Series fans in Candlestick Park were terrified as the stands swayed. [6] Fans were evacuated and there were reports of injuries. [7] The quake shook buildings in Sacramento, 95 miles to the northeast and was felt 225 miles to the east at Reno, NV. [8] Private groups and officials from around the world and the US have offered earthquake relief. [9] President Bush directed $273 million in aid. [10] Federal and private coffers are strained by previous response to Hurricane Hugo. [11] Some buildings in San Francisco collapsed because they were built over landfill or erected before there were earthquake codes. [12] The earthquake was the strongest on the San Andreas fault since the San Francisco quake of 1906. [13] Many scientists' studies led them to believe the next "big one" would be in Los Angles. [14] A 1988 US Geological Survey study predicted the area where the quake actually occurred.
