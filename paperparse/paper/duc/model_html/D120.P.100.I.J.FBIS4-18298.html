[1] On Easter Sunday, three Cabinet ministers supported John Major in the wake of speculation that he could be forced out this autumn. [2] Both Secretary of State for Education John Patten and Michael Howard, the Home Secretary, dismissed talk of a leadership contest before the next general election. [3] Lord Wakeham, Leader of the House of Lords, called on Torries to be loyal to Mr. Major. [4] One poll shows that Michael Heseltine is the favorite to replace Mr. [5] Major if he is forced out, but that Michael Portillo will vigorously campaign to secure Eurosceptic policies from the eventual winner.
