[1] A major earthquake lasting about fifteen seconds and measuring 6.5 on the Richter scale, rocked northern California on Tuesday evening. [2] One section of the San Francisco Bay Bridge collapsed. [3] Nearby buildings swayed several feet and some may have collapsed. [4] Phones and power were knocked out. [5] Fans at Candlestick Park waiting for the World Series to begin screamed as the stadium swayed for about fifteen seconds. [6] Fans were evacuated and there were reports of some injuries. [7] The quake shook buildings in Sacramento, 95 miles to the northeast, and was felt as far away as Reno, NV, 225 miles to the east.
