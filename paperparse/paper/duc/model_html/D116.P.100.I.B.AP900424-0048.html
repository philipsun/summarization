[1] With the Hubble Space Telescope about to be launched, astronomers are again excited about what it might accomplish. [2] It will scan the outermost reaches of space for clues about the origin and possible end of the universe. [3] Its launch has been delayed since 1983 by technical problems and the Challenger accident. [4] At an altitude of 380 miles, it will orbit far above earth's distorting atmosphere. [5] During its 15-year working lifetime, the telescope will provide astronomers with 10 times better resolution and 25 times more sensitivity than ground-based observatories. [6] Its use, shared with the European Space Agency, will cost about $200 million per year.
