[1] An Arab League Council meeting of foreign ministers was convened in Cairo at Kuwait's request. [2] The Council failed to publicly condemn Iraq's invasion of Kuwait. [3] So far, only Lebanon's prime minister and foreign minister, Salim Hoss, has joined Kuwait to publicly condemn this invasion--the first time in modern history one Arab country invaded another to take control of the government. [4] Iraq claims it entered Kuwait at the request of a Kuwaiti revolutionary group that had overthrown the government. [5] Kuwait has invoked the 1950 Arab joint defense pact, which requires pact members "to repel [invader] aggression and restore security and peace".
