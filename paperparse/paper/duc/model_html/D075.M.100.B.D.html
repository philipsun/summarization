[1] New treatments are contributing to fewer deaths from heart attacks over the past decade. [2] Drugs, surgery, and tests to determine risk all show promise. [3] Taking aspirin can cut heart attack risk in half, but can increase the risk of hemorrhagic stroke in some people. [4] Post- heart drugs dissolve clots and limit the damage to cardiac cells reducing the risk of a subsequent heart attack. [5] Angioplasty may be needed for fewer heart attack victims with the use of these drugs. [6] Surgery bypassing one-third of the lower intestine lowers cholesterol and tests to determine levels of a blood protein can identify those at risk for a heart attack.
