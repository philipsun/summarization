[1] Nepalese police have bulldozed more than 50 brick and mud houses that have been made unstable by an earthquake that struck near the India-Nepal border and killed at least 749 people. [2] Police said they will continue demolishing houses until satisfied that those remaining are safe to live in. [3] Most homeless have moved in with neighbors or found shelter in tents while waiting to hear from the government about compensation or new homes. [4] Prime Minister Shrestha while visiting Nepal's hardest hit town, Dharan, on Tuesday said, "This is the biggest calamity to strike Nepal". [5] The quake registered 6.5 on the Richter scale.
