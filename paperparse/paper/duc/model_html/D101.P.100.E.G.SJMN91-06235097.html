[1] President Mikhail Gorbachev furiously denounced the plotters who briefly overthrew him but said their failure could be an "enormous opportunity" to further his democratic reforms. [2] He detailed his purge of the coup leaders and pledged cooperation with and praised Russian Republic President Boris Yeltsin and said he did not plan to leave the Communist Party. [3] While in confinement in Crimea during the ouster, Gorbachev said his captors cut off all communications and his only source of news was foreign broadcasts. [4] He refused demands that he resign and prepared himself and his family for the possibility they would be killed.
