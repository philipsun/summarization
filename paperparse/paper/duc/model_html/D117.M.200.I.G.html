[1] America has the Pulitzer Prize for fiction and the National Book Awards but in terms of hoopla and excitement, there's nothing akin to Britain's Booker Prize. [2] British publishers each submit three new titles for a total of approximately 100. [3] There's a jury of judges, including writers, academics, politicians, editors and critics. [4] Each juror reads every book and odds are given with reviews. [5] The jurors then meet and select six finalists. [6] Literary insiders bet on which book will win the Booker Prize. [7] The winner is announced at a banquet in London's Elizabethan Guildhall, the seat the city's Lord Mayor, on the last Thursday in October. [8] The authors can win up to $38,000. [9] The event also guarantees great prestige and sales for the winner and brings the novel to public attention. [10] Critics say the brouhaha distorts the annual fiction industry and the intense media hype does a disservice to other gifted but unlisted authors. [11] On one recent occasion following heated deliberation between the judges, the chairman went public with a rousing condemnation of contemporary fiction. [12] The Prize, first awarded in 1969, is administered by Book Trust, an educational charity promoting books. [13] It is sponsored by Booker, an international food and farming business.
