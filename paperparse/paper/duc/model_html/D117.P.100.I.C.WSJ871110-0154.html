[1] Penelope Lively won the 1987 Booker Prize. [2] She won for "Moon Tigers", a dying woman's recollection of and her work as a WW II correspondent and her affair with a young officer. [3] The Booker is awarded to the author of the best work of fiction published in Great Britain during the preceding 12 months. [4] In late September the judges announce a short-list of six contenders chosen from the publishers' submissions. [5] The competition is very popular and elicits heavy betting. [6] Odds on Lively were seven-to-one. [7] The Booker was first awarded in 1969. [8] The idea was to devise a British equivalent to the French Prix Goncourt.
