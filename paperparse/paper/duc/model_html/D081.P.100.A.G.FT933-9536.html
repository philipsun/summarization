[1] Coal miners from Romania's Jiu Valley who led anti-reform rampages through Bucharest in 1990 and 1991 are again on strike. [2] The government has not caved in and has begun legal action to have the strike declared illegal. [3] The miners' position seems untenable. [4] Romanians are opposed to the miners' wage demand, the mines are not considered a vital source of energy, and the miners are in danger of pricing their coal out of the market. [5] The miners want more money to compensate for difficult living and working conditions. [6] They also are following Miron Cosma, their charismatic leader who secured their earlier wage increases.
