[1] Discovery's astronauts sent the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) floating free from the shuttle today and it began orbiting Earth every 97.3 minutes at an altitude of 380 miles. [2] Ground controllers had difficulty unfurling two 39-foot solar panels which provide the power to recharge the telescope's batteries but succeeded on a third try. [3] Earlier this morning the Discovery commander maneuvered the shuttle into its deployment position to put the HST's solar arrays in the proper position to quickly start collecting energy. [4] The HST is 43 feet long, weighs 24,250 pounds and is about the size of a railroad tank car.
