[1] Emperor Hirohito of Japan has been bedridden for a month with an intestinal hemorrhage. [2] He has received nearly 22 pints of blood since his illness worsened and the transfusions put a strain on his heart and kidneys. [3] Recently his blood pressure and pulse dipped sharply, but then returned to normal even as the internal bleeding continued. [4] He is being fed intravenously and while complaining of fatigue does not appear to be in pain. [5] He visited with his two sons and was described as fully conscious and alert. [6] Princess Michiko, Hirohito's daughter-in-law, scaled down celebrations for her 54th birthday.
