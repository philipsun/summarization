[1] A severe drought continues to affect large areas of the United States and climatologists warn that the future could see more unless steps are taken to halt the earth's rising temperature. [2] The drought is the worst since records began being kept in 1895 and has affected grain production across the nation. [3] Grain supplies are expected to fall to 17 percent of what is required to safeguard world food security. [4] Communities in the areas affected have restricted water use and Oregon has begun to ship hay to Kentucky. [5] Some areas have seen some relief as heavy storms hit large sections of the country.
