[1] The official death toll in Sunday's pre-dawn Himalayan earthquake is 981, 704 in Nepal and 277 in India. [2] Many, especially in Dharan, Nepal, died when brick houses that used rain loosened mud between the bricks, collapsed in the 6.5 (Richter scale) quake. [3] Bihar, India, where authorities say 200 died, is also threatened by flooding. [4] Monsoon rains and aftershocks hampered relief efforts and the search for victims throughout the quake area. [5] Of Nepal's 157 Peace Corps volunteers, 154 have been found safe. [6] India has no Peace Corp workers. [7] The U.S., Britain, and the international community are sending aid to the devastated area.
