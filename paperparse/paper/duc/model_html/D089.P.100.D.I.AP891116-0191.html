[1] Tornados and severe thunderstorms have swept through the eastern half of the United States in the last two days, killing at least 27 people, injuring more than 500, and leaving hundreds homeless. [2] The worst casualties occurred in Huntsville, Alabama, where 17 were killed, and Newburgh, New York, where at least seven children died. [3] A truck driver died in New Jersey, and one woman died in Illinois. [4] Injuries occurred in Georgia, Indiana, Kentucky, Ohio, and West Virginia. [5] Heavy damage, but with no deaths or injuries, was reported in the District of Columbia, Maryland, Delaware, Mississippi, Pennsylvania, and North and South Carolina.
