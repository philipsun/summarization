[1] The cities of Charleston, SC and San Francisco have similarities and differences when they were the scenes of two back-to-back disasters: Hurricane Hugo and the Bay Area earthquake. [2] Charleston had warning but San Francisco did not. [3] Charleston's neighborhoods were littered with debris, 80% of the buildings were damaged, and power was out. [4] San Francisco's streets were passable within a day and power was restored overnight. [5] Charleston, however, in contrast to San Francisco, could quickly assess structural damage and it lost no major economic area such as the Bay Bridge. [6] Both have experienced delays in disaster relief from the federal government.
