[1] A Finnish-registered freighter with a cargo of beer sank in high winds in the North Sea. [2] The sinking occurred off the Dutch coast near the island of Ameland. [3] A West German helicopter rescued the crew members from their life rafts. [4] All nine were safe. [5] The ship's departure point and destination were not known. [6] The Dutch Navy also responded to the scene, sending a plane and two lifeboats. [7] Early reports had said the ship was a passenger ferry, possibly Dutch. [8] Ferries and cargo ships frequent the shipping lanes between the coastal islands and the Dutch mainland.
