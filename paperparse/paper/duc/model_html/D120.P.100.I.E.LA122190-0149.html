[1] In a display of solidarity with U.S. policy on the Persian Gulf, Prime Minister John Major today announced that Iraqi troops have to withdraw completely from Kuwait. [2] Major said that they will have to be removed forcibly if President Saddam Hussein does not agree to a peaceful withdrawal. [3] Major rejected any idea of a partial pullback by Iraqi troops and compared the situation in Kuwait to that of a prison camp. [4] Although Major is as unyielding as his predecessor, Margaret Thatcher, he showed none of her abrasiveness. [5] Major's remarks boded well for his talks at Camp David with President George Bush.
