[1] Huntington, Indiana, population 17,000, hosted an exhibit in May honoring its most important resident ever, Vice-President Dan Quayle. [2] Quayle, 44 in February, grew up in a white, ranch-style house on Polk Street with parents James and Corinne and younger brother Chris. [3] He loves golf and played on the high school team. [4] In 1972 Quayle was an aide to Indiana Gov. Edgar Whitcomb. [5] Dan and wife Marilyn opened a joint law practice in Huntington. [6] His favorite restaurant is Nick's Kitchen. [7] Quayle is known as a nice, capable guy with good values, from a nice family that runs the local newspaper. [8] Quayle didn't attend the tribute, citing a busy schedule.
