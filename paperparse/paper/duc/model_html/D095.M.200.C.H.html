[1] On Tuesday, January 16, 1989, Patrick Edward Purdy entered the grounds of Cleveland Elementary School in Stockton, California and fired an AK-47 automatic assault rifle into a crowded playground. [2] He killed five children and wounded 29 others and a teacher, then killed himself. [3] All the dead and 19 of the wounded were children of Southeast Asian refugees, as were 60% of the school's 970 students. [4] Translators and Buddhist priests assisted families, who received financial aid from the county victim assistance program. [5] Students returning to school received counseling. [6] A South Carolina school where a similar attack occurred sent encouragement. [7] Governor Deukmejian spoke at one memorial service and was considering a ban on assault weapon sales. [8] Anti-racism statements were made but authorities denied a racial motive in the attack. [9] Purdy, AKA West, 24, blond and blue-eyed, attended Cleveland Elementary through third grade. [10] He dropped out of high school and took welding classes, then drifted around the country searching for work. [11] He had many LA area arrests for minor offenses, served jail time, and attempted suicide. [12] He had military and white supremacist leanings.
