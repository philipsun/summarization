[1] The death toll from flash flooding in creeks in eastern Ohio reached 19 Sunday. [2] Workers used picks and shovels to dig along creek banks where dogs had detected human scent. [3] Twenty-three dog teams from four states searched for victims. [4] Often, cars are found buried in the brush and tree piles and they had to be torn apart and searched separately. [5] The flood destroyed as many as 70 houses and damaged 40 others. [6] Officials learned that some of those feared missing had moved out of the area but that still left 18 listed as missing.
