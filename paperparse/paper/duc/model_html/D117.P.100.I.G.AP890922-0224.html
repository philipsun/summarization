[1] Six novels have been nominated for the Booker Prize, Britain's most prestigious fiction award. [2] Bookmakers say the favorite is Japanese author Kazuo Ishiguro's "The Remains of the Day" about a butler's travels through Britain's West Country. [3] An odds maker gave it 7 to 4 odds. [4] More interest in the award than ever before is expected because of last year's controversy over Salmon Rushdie's "Satanic Verses". [5] Rushdie lost out last year to Australian author Peter Carey for his "Oscar and Lucinda". [6] Rushdie, a British citizen, has been hiding since February 14 when the Iranian leader Khomeini ordered him killed for blaspheming Islam in "Satanic Verses".
