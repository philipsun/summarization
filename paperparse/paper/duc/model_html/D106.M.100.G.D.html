[1] Nelson Mandela, the leading opponent of South Africa's white-led government was freed after 27 years in prison on 11 February 1990. [2] From prison he had led the fight against the government's policy of racial segregation, apartheid. [3] He was convicted in 1964 of conspiracy to overthrow the government and sentenced to life in prison. [4] International pressure had been mounting for his release and many governments had imposed sanctions on South Africa. [5] He continued the fight for black human rights after his release, but had many problems to overcome including factional fighting between the ANC and the Zulu Inkatha group.
