[1] An experiment indicates that a blood and urine test of individuals with high blood pressure can pinpoint those patients most likely to have a heart attack. [2] The test also can determine those patients whose risk of heart attack is extremely low. [3] The test determines whether the kidneys are releasing too much renin into the blood. [4] People with high levels of rennin are at a high risk of heart attack. [5] The test can be especially useful for determining heart attack risks in patients without any of the usual risk factors. [6] Those in this group with high rennin levels were seven times more likely to suffer a heart attack.
