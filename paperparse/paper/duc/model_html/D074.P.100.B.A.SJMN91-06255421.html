[1] Virginia Thomas, the white wife of black Supreme Court nominee Clarence Thomas, is a brainy lawyer with an impressive career. [2] Critics see her as a woman with strong opinions on issues that are bound to come before the court. [3] Women's-rights activists cite her opposition to comparable-worth legislation and some religious groups dislike her anti-cult activities. [4] Some blacks feel the Thomases' marriage shows his rejection of the black community. [5] But in the eyes of the Thomases they have stood for true equality and rejected programs that actually harm women and minorities by lowering standards. [6] They advocate and live their belief in a colorblind society.
