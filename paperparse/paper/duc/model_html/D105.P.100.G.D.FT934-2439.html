[1] The international community is becoming increasingly aware of the true magnitude of ethnic and political conflicts in the former Soviet Union. [2] Russia is concerned about conflicts close to its borders, about the safety of ethnic Russians living in those areas and about refugees fleeing to Russia. [3] They argue that the international community should undertake peacekeeping missions in these areas as they have in other hotspots. [4] These areas should be of concern to the international community both for humanitarian reasons and because they risk provoking regional instability. [5] If such peacekeeping missions are undertaken, respect for the sovereignty of the newly independent countries must remain paramount.
