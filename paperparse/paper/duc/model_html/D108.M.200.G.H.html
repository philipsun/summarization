[1] On August 2nd, 1990, 100,000 Iraqi troops massing on Kuwait's border crossed over, occupying the country and destroying all government buildings and installations except for the water system and oil equipment. [2] Over 200 Kuwaitis were killed or wounded. [3] Iraq facilitated residents' departures then trucked in Iraqis to replace Kuwaitis and change the population's make-up. [4] Thousands fled to Saudi Arabia, including the leadership. [5] Jordan also took in refugees. [6] Saddam Hussain accused Kuwait of exceeding OPEC production quotas to drive down prices, and stealing oil from Iraqi border wells. [7] Iraq claimed it invaded at the request of Kuwaiti revolutionaries who staged a coup, which Kuwait denied. [8] Kuwait said Iraq invaded because it demanded Iraq pay back billions loaned during the 1980-1988 Iran- Iraq War. [9] Iran rejoiced that the world was waking up to Saddam Hussein. [10] Kuwait appealed for US and UN help. [11] Bush said the US would take any action necessary to defend its Persian Gulf interests and diverted a naval group to the Gulf. [12] The US, UN, and EEC imposed trade and military sanctions against Iraq and Kuwait, supported widely by other countries. [13] The Soviets and China stopped Iraqi arms sales. [14] Iraq rounded up Westerners in Kuwait and threatened to hold hostage citizens of countries imposing sanctions. [15] Oil prices hit five-year highs.
