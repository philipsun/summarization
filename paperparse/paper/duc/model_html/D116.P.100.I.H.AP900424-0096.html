[1] Five hours after shuttle Discovery's launch Tuesday carrying the Hubble Space Telescope, delayed since 1983, Hubble sent its first signal. [2] Hubble is hailed as the greatest advance in astronomy since Galileo. [3] In its 15 years orbiting 380 miles above Earth, its two cameras, two spectrometers, photometer, fine-pointing system, and 94.5- and 12-inch mirrors can look 14 billion years backward in time. [4] Tomorrow, Discovery's mechanical arm will lift the 12-ton, 43-foot long, 14-foot wide Hubble out of the cargo bay. [5] Batteries will power Hubble until wing-like solar panels extend, with manual help if necessary. [6] The $1.5 billion telescope cost $7 million a month to maintain on Earth.
