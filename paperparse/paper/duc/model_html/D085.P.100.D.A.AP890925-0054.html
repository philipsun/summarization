[1] The mayors of Charleston and North Charleston, S.C. gave credit to residents of their cities for the low fatality rate from Hurricane Hugo which hit the city with full force. [2] Although the hurricane claimed 17 lives in South Carolina, Charleston, which took the brunt of the storm, lost only two. [3] The residents heard the warnings of forecasters and officials and took action upon them. [4] 18,000 residents of Charleston and the three surrounding counties headed for shelters. [5] Tens of thousands more got into their cars and headed inland. [6] All of these potential victims saved themselves.
