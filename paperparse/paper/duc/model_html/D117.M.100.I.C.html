[1] The Booker Prize was established in 1969 to honor the best work of fiction by a British or Commonwealth author. [2] It is sponsored by Booker, an international food and farming business. [3] The Booker carries a substantial monetary award, and is now Britain's most prestigious fiction prize. [4] Judges name six finalists, and later select the winner from those six. [5] The competition creates great interest and motivates bettors. [6] It also boosts book sales. [7] Three of the recent winners now live in Britain or a Commonwealth country, but were born elsewhere. [8] Only books written in English can be entered into Booker competition.
