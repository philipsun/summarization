[1] Mt. Pinatubo erupted on 9 June 1991 for the first time in 600 years. [2] Further explosions continued for five days, with as many as six in a single day. [3] Philippine and U.S. experts said that a more massive eruption could occur. [4] The mountain shot out gases, rock and ash. [5] Clouds ascending from Pinatubo rose to heights of ten miles, turning day into night and covering wide areas in ash. [6] Personnel from the large U.S. facility at Clark Air Base were evacuated to the naval base at Subic Bay, most of them soon after the first eruption.
