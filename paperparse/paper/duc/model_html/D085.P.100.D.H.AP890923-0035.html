[1] With unchecked carbon dioxide and methane gas emissions, world temperatures could rise 10 degrees and the greenhouse effect could breed hurricanes 50% stronger than Hugo, which blasted the Caribbean and Carolinas last week with 135mph winds. [2] Last year's Gilbert, with winds reaching 180mph, is the most powerful hurricane recorded and seems the upper limit with current ocean temperatures. [3] But if seas warm up 3-6 degrees, winds could be 25% higher with 50% more destructive force. [4] Hugo demonstrated the effect of warm water when its winds jumped from 105 to 135mph as it passed over the Gulf Stream. [5] The hurricane intensity scale may have to be raised to 6.
