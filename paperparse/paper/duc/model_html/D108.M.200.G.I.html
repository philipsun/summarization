[1] The Iraqi occupation of Kuwait began on August 2 when masses of combat-experienced troops poured across the border, quickly taking the country. [2] The Kuwait emir fled to Saudi Arabia. [3] The dispute began in mid-July when Iraq, a nation with 17 million, accused tiny Kuwait of stealing oil and flooding the market. [4] As Iraqi troops mobilized, efforts to avoid a confrontation failed. [5] While Iraqi said it invaded at the request of Kuwaiti revolutionaries, a Kuwaiti spokesman called this a false pretext. [6] President Bush said he would defend U.S. interests in the Persian Gulf. [7] Iran, which fought an 8-year war with Iraq, rejoiced that the world saw the "Frankenstein" created by the United States and Kuwait. [8] The United Nations Security Council unanimously passed trade and military sanctions against Iraq and Kuwait. [9] Most nations strongly enforced these sanctions, as oil prices soared. [10] Iraq rounded-up foreign nationals in Kuwait, including passengers of a British airliner, and took many to Iraq. [11] An Iraqi jet finally brought some to Jordan on August 7. [12] Refugees poured into Saudi Arabia. [13] Some reported that Iraqis were moving in and being given Kuwaiti identities. [14] Iraq began evacuation drills and warned of U.S. or Israeli actions.
