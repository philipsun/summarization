[1] Doctors should be cautious in prescribing aspirin to prevent heart attacks until more research supports its value. [2] Studies do indicate that aspirin reduces the risk of first non- fatal heart attacks, by reducing the ability of blood platelets to form clots. [3] But for people who have clotting problems or have high blood pressure aspirin may increase the risk for hemorrhagic stroke. [4] In one study, there were 10 hemorrhagic strokes recorded among the 11,000 subjects taking aspirin, compared with 2 strokes in the 11,000 taking a substitute. [5] Overall death rates for the two groups were similar, though the aspirin non-fatal heart attack rate was much lower.
