[1] The San Francisco 49ers' 55-10 rout of the Denver Broncos was the lowest rated Super Bowl since 1969 and the third-lowest ever. [2] The CBS game averaged a 39.0 rating and a 63 share. [3] Each point in the rating represents 921,000 homes. [4] The share is the percentage of the televisions on at the time. [5] Despite the low rating, about 108.5 million people saw Sunday's game, making it the ninth most-watched TV show ever in the U.S. behind eight Super Bowls and the final episode of "Mash". [6] The annual increase in the number of homes with television accounted for the higher viewership.
