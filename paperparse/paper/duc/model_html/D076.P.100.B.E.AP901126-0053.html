[1] Margaret Thatcher broke the mold in English politics when she became that country's first woman Prime Minister. [2] Even so, critics say she did little to advance the cause of women. [3] No believer in feminism, Mrs. Thatcher believed the battle for women's rights had been won. [4] Women occupy 43 percent of the British workforce yet they generally are paid less and are poorly represented in managerial jobs and parliament. [5] Supporters point to Mrs. [6] Thatcher's legislation on work place nurseries and independent taxation for working women. [7] As prime minister, she angered feminists by emphasizing women's roles as homemakers and care givers.
