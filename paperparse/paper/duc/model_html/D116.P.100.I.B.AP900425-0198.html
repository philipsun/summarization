[1] The $1.5 billion Hubble Space Telescope left the shuttle Discovery's cargo bay on Wednesday, nearly two hours and one orbit late, for orbit 380 statute miles above the earth's distorting atmosphere. [2] It took three attempts to deploy the telescope's second solar array panel, thus causing the delayed release from Discovery to operational orbit. [3] Suited astronauts were standing by to manually unfurl the solar panels if necessary. [4] Next, after first testing the telescope's maneuverability, the 10-foot aperture door will be opened on Friday, exposing the finely polished 94.5-inch mirror to starlight. [5] NASA expects its first images from space on Tuesday.
