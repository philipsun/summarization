[1] A strong earthquake hit Nepal and parts of eastern India on Sunday, triggering landslides and collapsing buildings. [2] First reports said 100 people may have died. [3] Nepal's Department of Geology said the earthquake measured 5.7 on the Richter scale. [4] The quake, lasting about 40 seconds, was centered near Udayapur, a town near the Indian- Nepalese border and 100 miles southeast of Katmandu, Nepal's capital. [5] The quake was also felt in the Indian capital of New Delhi and Calcutta, but there were no reports of casualties. [6] In New Delhi, the Seismological Observatory said the quake measured 6.5 on the Richter scale.
