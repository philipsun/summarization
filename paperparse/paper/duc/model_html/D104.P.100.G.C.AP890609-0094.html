[1] More than 5,000 people packed a Chinatown park in New York City Saturday morning for a memorial service honoring the demonstrators killed last weekend in Tiananmen Square. [2] The crowd wave banners and wore buttons honoring "the martyrs of Tiananmen Square". [3] After the service and speeches, the crowd, led by a casket and Chinese funeral bier, marched through Chinatown in a procession that stretched for blocks. [4] One of the speakers said the violent suppression of the democratic reformers had united Chinese everywhere. [5] Demonstrators planned another rally later Saturday outside the United Nations building.
