[1] As vice president, Dan Quayle is likely to be the "man on the outside" in the Bush White House. [2] His image is that of political novice and during the campaign he was relegated to small towns and safe GOP areas. [3] Bush rarely mentioned him while campaigning. [4] Quayle has said that he will be a close advisor to Bush but few believe that will happen. [5] Bush and Walter Mondale in their VP campaigns had placed key staffers in their respective presidential campaigns. [6] Quayle has contributed nothing in the way of staff to Bush's campaign. [7] Quayle is apparently adapting to his anticipated role and anonymity.
