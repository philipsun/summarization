[1] Jurors cited taped interviews by the Children's Institute International as a reason for acquitting Raymond Buckey and his mother, Peggy McMartin Buckey, on 52 charges of child molestation. [2] The jurors felt that CII interviewers asked leading questions and never allowed the children to tell the story in their own words; contaminating the evidence. [3] Jurors said they couldn't tell if the children believed what they were saying or were repeating what parents or CII interviewers had told them. [4] The jurors deadlocked on 13 other charges. [5] While unsure if Buckey was a molester, jurors were sure that the prosecution had failed to prove the molestation allegations.
