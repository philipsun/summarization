[1] On Friday, Nepal updated its death toll from Sunday's Himalayan earthquake to 704 as reports arrived from remote area. [2] In India, the official death toll rose to 277, 200 of which were in Bihar, but the press and others claim as many as 1500 may have perished. [3] Bihar is also threatened by flooding from swollen rivers. [4] The quake, measuring 6.5 on the Richter scale, struck at 4:54 a.m. [5] (local). According to Nepal's Department of Geology, it was centered near Udayapur, Nepal. [6] Monsoon rains, overflowing rivers, submerged bridges and roads and aftershocks hampered relief efforts and the search for victims in throughout the quake area. [7] In Dharan, Nepal, over 131 bodies were found, most in collapsed houses that had used mud, loosened by rain, between the bricks. [8] Karna Hyanju, a former Nepalese legislator, however, did not die in the quake. [9] He was killed after being accused of distributing relief supplies only to his supporters. [10] All but three of Nepal's 157 Peace Corps volunteers have been found safe. [11] There are no Peace Corp workers in India. [12] The U.S. is sending financial aid to both counties. [13] Britain, U.N. agencies and the Red Cross sent medical teams to Nepal.
