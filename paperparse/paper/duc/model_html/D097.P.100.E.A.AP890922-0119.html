[1] As Hurricane Hugo's 135 mph wind roared outside, Charleston S.C.'s mayor Joseph P. Riley, city officials and a few reporters stayed in Charleston's City Hall watching the storm's fury. [2] The building's tin roof ripped off and water seeped down into the City Council chamber. [3] Phone and electric service to the city command post was knocked out. [4] When the eye of the storm passed, Riley took reporters out of the 188-year-old building to look at the damage. [5] The tin roof was in the gutter, slate tiles were scattered on the ground. [6] Bricks, sticks and leaves littered the street. [7] "It's scarcely a late summer breeze," he commented.
