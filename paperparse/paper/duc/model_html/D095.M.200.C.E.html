[1] Patrick Edward Purdy, a drifter with a troubled past, returned to the elementary school where he attended kindergarten through third grade and killed five children. [2] Armed with an AK-47 and a pistol with victory written on it, Purdy went to Cleveland Elementary School in Stockton and opened fire in the crowded schoolyard at recess. [3] Firing over 100 rounds in a few minutes, Purdy killed five students and wounded 29 others as well as a teacher. [4] He then committed suicide with the pistol. [5] Purdy was described by his aunt as a loner and a child alcoholic. [6] He had a long history of trouble with the law and had served at least two jail terms. [7] Cleveland Elementary serves the third largest Asian refugee community in California and the five students killed as well as 19 of the 29 students were Asian. [8] One of those killed, a Vietnamese student, was buried Saturday following a Catholic mass. [9] Traditional Buddhist prayers were being held for three of the four Cambodian students slain. [10] A community service is being planned at the Stockton Civic Center on Monday. [11] Governor Deukmejian, who will be attending the event, has asked that everyone wear black and white ribbons in keeping with Southeast Asian tradition.
