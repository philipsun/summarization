[1] A United States Coast Guard ship on patrol spotted a man and woman in a raft about 30 miles off the coast of Costa Rica. [2] Several whales had attacked and sunk their pleasure boat Sibonay about 1,200 miles southwest of Costa Rica. [3] They survived 66 days in their rubber life raft by eating raw fish and drinking purified water. [4] A Coast Guard officer wasn't sure if there were any documented cases of whale aggression. [5] The couple, William and Simone Butler, are resting in a Costa Rican hospital. [6] William Butler is an experienced sailor. [7] They were sailing from Coral Gables to Honolulu.
