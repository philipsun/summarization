[1] Iraq, a country of more than 17 million, using 100,000 troops and 350 tanks invaded and quickly conquered Kuwait, an oil-rich state, with 1.9 million residents--60 percent of them foreigners--and an army of 20,300. [2] Iraq, after accusing Kuwait of much wrongdoing, claimed it invaded at the request of Kuwaiti revolutionaries. [3] The U.N. [4] Security Council condemned the invasion. [5] Kuwait's U.S. ambassador asked for American military intervention. [6] President Bush froze all Iraqi assets in the U.S., blocked Iraqi imports and, for their protection, froze Kuwaiti assets under U.S. jurisdiction. [7] The Pentagon diverted a naval group toward the Gulf. [8] Oil prices soared.
