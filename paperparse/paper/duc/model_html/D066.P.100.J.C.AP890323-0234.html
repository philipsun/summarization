[1] Samuel Morse Walton, son of a farm mortgage broker, was born in Oklahoma on 29 March 1918. [2] He earned an economics degree from Missouri University and worked for J.C. [3] Penney's as a management trainee. [4] After a three year stint in the army Walton opened a Ben Franklin franchise, and eventually became the largest franchisee. [5] He and a brother opened the first Wal-Mart Discount City in 1962. [6] The brother is still a board member, and Sam's family is involved in the business. [7] In 1987 Forbes estimated the family's stake in the business at about 6.8 billion dollars.
