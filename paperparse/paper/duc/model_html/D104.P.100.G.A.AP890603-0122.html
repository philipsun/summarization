[1] On Saturday at 1 a.m. tens of thousands of troops entered Beijing from the east marching toward Tiananmen Square. [2] Blocked east of the square by thousands of people, the troops dispersed, walking around near the square in small groups. [3] About noon two truckloads of People's Armed Police attacked crowds east of the square. [4] Later about 5,000 troops appeared firing tear gas and clubbing protesters west of the square. [5] Skirmishes continued until 9 p.m. when troops started firing at the rock-throwing protesters. [6] Shooting continued through the night until 5 a.m. Sunday morning when the demonstrators, surrounded by armed and firing troops, began leaving the square, ending their three-week occupation.
