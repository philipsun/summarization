[1] Japanese writer Kazuo Ishiguro won the 1989 Booker Prize, Britain's top literary award, for his novel, "The Remains of the Day", a love story about a butler's English vacation. [2] It explores the vexed themes of class, tradition, and duty. [3] Ishiguro, 39, arrived in England at age 6 and now lives in south London. [4] He was a finalist for the Booker Prize in 1986. [5] After receiving the prize, Ishiguro paid tribute to Salmon Rushdie, the 1988 runner-up for the award, who went into hiding in February when threatened with death by Moslems who said his novel, "The Satanic Verses" blasphemed their faith.
