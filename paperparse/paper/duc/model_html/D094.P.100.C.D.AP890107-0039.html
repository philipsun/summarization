[1] World leaders expressed condolences at the death of Japan's 87-year-old Emperor Hirohito, who surrendered his country at the end of World War II, but went on to preside over its economic rise. [2] Several Asian countries were decidedly cool however, noting the suffering of their people at the hands of the Japanese army during the war. [3] The Korean president referred to Japan and Korea's unhappy past. [4] Taiwan newspapers remarked that Chinese people suffered the most from Japan's invasion. [5] President Reagan referred to his reign as spanning a tumultuous and yet constructive era. [6] The Philippine foreign secretary praised Hirohito as a stabilizing factor in post-war Japan.
