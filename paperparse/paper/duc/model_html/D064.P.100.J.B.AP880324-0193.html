[1] Communism suffered its first Big Mac attack Thursday as McDonald's opened a restaurant in Belgrade, Yugoslavia. [2] This, its first outlet in a communist country, has 350 seats, employs 110 people and is capable of serving 2,500 meals per hour. [3] Police kept an eye on eager customers who waited in line for hours to get in. [4] The McDonald's hamburgers, fries and shakes will compete with a local favorite fast-food dish, the pljeskavica, ground pork and onions on a bun. [5] In a change to Yugoslav eating customs, McDonalds will not serve alcohol or allow smoking, nor allow customers to linger long after their meal is finished.
