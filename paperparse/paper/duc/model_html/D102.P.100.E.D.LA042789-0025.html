[1] Lucille Ball, TV's comic genius, died at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center at the age of 77 following emergency heart surgery. [2] She earned her niche in television history in the comedy "I Love Lucy", in which her husband, Desi Arnaz also starred. [3] The weekly show which premiered in 1951, had a phenomenal 40 million viewers and department stores even changed the night they remained open to accommodate the show. [4] In addition to being a talented entertainer, Miss Ball was a shrewd businesswoman who became along with her husband, head of one of Hollywood's major studios. [5] The couple divorced in 1960.
