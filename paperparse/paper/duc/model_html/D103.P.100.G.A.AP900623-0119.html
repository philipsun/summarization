[1] Countries around the world, both friendly and hostile to Irans' Islamic government, sent help to Iran where 40,000 people died in the earthquake Thursday. [2] The United States expressed its willingness to help and Iran said it would welcome aid from the Red Cross and other U.S. humanitarian groups. [3] Moderate Arab countries-- Egypt, Saudi Arabia, and Jordan--offered help as did Italy, France, West Germany, the Vatican, India, China, Poland and Malaysia. [4] The Soviet republic of Armenia flew a professional lifesaving team to Iran on Saturday. [5] Iran's neighbor, Soviet Azerbaijan, sent tons of food and contributed more than $500,000 to a special earthquake fund.
