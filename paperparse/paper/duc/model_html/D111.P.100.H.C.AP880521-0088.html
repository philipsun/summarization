[1] Greek divers located the remains of a wooden sailing vessel on 3 May 1988. [2] The vessel was found near Chios, a Greek island in the eastern Aegean Sea, where a Greek naval vessel sank a Turkish warship on 7 June 1882. [3] The Turkish vessel was identified as the Bourloti Seimaz, or translated as "Explosives Invincible". [4] It was returning from raiding and killing thousands of Greeks on Chios. [5] More than 1,000, including 400 Greeks, were aboard the Turkish ship. [6] The ship was crammed with loot taken from Chios. [7] The Greek divers hope to interest American and Turkish experts to help in recovering the vessel.
