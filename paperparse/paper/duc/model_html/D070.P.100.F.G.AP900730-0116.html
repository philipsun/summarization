[1] East Germany's deposed Communist leader, Erich Honecker, has been declared too sick to be in jail but fit enough to be tried. [2] He is still recovering from kidney cancer surgery in January. [3] A medical panel concluded that seven other former Communist officials--all close aides to Honecker--were fit to prosecute and to be placed in custody despite advanced age and poor health. [4] Along with Honecker, they are charged with corruption and abuse of office. [5] Honecker has been staying at a Soviet military hospital outside East Berlin. [6] Although their poor health often disrupted proceedings, investigations against the former East German leaders will continue.
