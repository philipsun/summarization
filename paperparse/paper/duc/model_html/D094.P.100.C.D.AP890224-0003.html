[1] To the rest of the world, he was known as Emperor Hirohito, but in Japan his name was rarely used. [2] Japanese found the foreign practice of using his given name impolite and referred to him simply as His majesty, the Emperor. [3] Japanese histories will record him as Emperor Showa, the name he chose when he became emperor in 1926. [4] His son, Akihito succeeded Emperor Hirohito and has chosen the name Heisei. [5] Japan numbers the years according to the emperor's tenure on the thrown. [6] Thus the first year of Hirohito's reign was Showa 1. [7] Until he died, 1989 was Showa 64, now it is Heisei 1.
