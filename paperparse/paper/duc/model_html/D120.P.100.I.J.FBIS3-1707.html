[1] In a welcome to British Prime Minister John Major today at the Pittsburgh airport, President Clinton said the two nations were working together to end the conflict in Bosnia. [2] Major, after visiting Pittsburgh where his father and grandfather once lived, will travel with the President back to Washington on Air Force One. [3] On Tuesday, the two leaders will begin discussing a wide range of issues including Russia, Bosnia, Northern Ireland, and world trade. [4] Clinton and Major will meet again in June during D-Day 50th anniversary events in Europe. [5] Major said Clinton would visit Britain, including possibly Oxford University, Clinton's alma mater.
