[1] Sam Walton's phenomenal success as a retailer stemmed from his personality, and his willingness to defy accepted retailing wisdom. [2] By 1991 Wal-Mart was the world's largest retailer, as measured by revenues. [3] His success made him very wealthy, but he did not take on the trappings of the opulent. [4] Employee satisfaction was very important to him, and he instituted policies to keep them happy. [5] He put his stores in small rural towns despite warnings that they would fail. [6] They were a great success but often caused the failure of many small businesses.
