[1] The 1988 Summer Olympics in Seoul had no terrorist acts but was marred by drug use, most notably by Canadian sprinter Ben Johnson, and bad behavior by a few athletes. [2] The Soviets won the most medals, followed by the East Germans and then the US. [3] Because of the boycotts in 1980 and 1984, these games were the first meeting of the US and USSR since 1972. [4] Only Communist North Korea and sports power Cuba boycotted these games. [5] A estimated billion people watched the games on a worldwide TV hookup. [6] The Seoul games had the greatest number of athletes ever to compete in the Olympics.
