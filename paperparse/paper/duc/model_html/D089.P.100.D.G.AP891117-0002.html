[1] Federal aid was approved Friday for tornado-ravaged Huntsville, AL where 17 died, more than 400 were injured, 80 in hospital care, and about 1,000 left homeless. [2] 119 houses were destroyed, 125 had minor damage, and three churches, two schools, 35 businesses and 10 public buildings were either destroyed or suffered heavy damage. [3] Tornado winds were estimated at 250 mph. [4] State officials estimated the damage in excess of $100 million. [5] About $1 million may be needed for clothing and temporary housing. [6] The federal government pays 75% and the state 25%. [7] Federal checks are expected to be on the way within the week.
