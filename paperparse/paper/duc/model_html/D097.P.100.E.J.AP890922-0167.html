[1] National Hurricane Center forecasters used computer models to track Hugo's path into Charleston, giving people time to evacuate the South Carolina coast. [2] A greater problem was predicting the intensity of the storm, which was upgraded to a Category 4 hurricane just hours before it hit Charleston. [3] The increase in wind speed, which was detected by Air Force reconnaissance, surprised hurricane specialists. [4] Satellite information is fed to supercomputers at the National Meteorological Center in Suitland, Md. which send data to the hurricane center's tracking model. [5] MIT meteorology professor Kerry Emmanuel criticizes the system because satellites don't provide sufficient wind and temperature data.
