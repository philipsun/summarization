[1] A one-day strike by coal miners in Kazakhstan and the Ukraine has grown into a nationwide walkout involving up to 100,000 miners demanding that President Gorbachev surrender power to the restive republics. [2] Most of the country's 1.2 million coal miners are still working, but output in some key regions has been halved. [3] The strike is the new union's first major test of strength. [4] It began as a warning in Kazakhstan and the Ukraine but gradually spread to all of the country's major regions. [5] Previous strikes have made only economic demands. [6] This time the miners want political concessions.
