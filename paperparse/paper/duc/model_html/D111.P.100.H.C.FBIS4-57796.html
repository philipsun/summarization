[1] The Korean-flagged fishing vessel Chin Yuan Hsing was scuttled in waters off Argentina on 21 May 1994. [2] Adverse weather conditions prevailed when the Argentine corvette Spiro spotted the Korean ship fishing illegally in restricted Argentine waters. [3] The Korean vessel did not respond to the corvette's warning. [4] Instead, the Korean crew sank their ship, and transferred to another Korean ship sailing nearby. [5] In accordance with a UK-Argentine agreement, the Argentine Navy turned the surveillance of the second Korean vessel over to the British when it approached within 15 nautical miles of the Malvinas (Falkland) Islands.
