[1] The 1990 San Diego Marathon was run on the North County course. [2] It was the first marathon to be run there. [3] Contestants liked the new course. [4] The marathon was run in beautiful weather, with warm breezes and 74-degree readings. [5] Over 4,000 runners participated, but the field was considered to be a weak one. [6] Benjamin Martinez of Mexico was the men's winner. [7] Kathy Smith of Newport Beach was the fastest woman. [8] Each received two thousand dollars for the victory. [9] Both are relatively new to running the marathon distance of 26.2 miles, and for each it was the first marathon victory.
