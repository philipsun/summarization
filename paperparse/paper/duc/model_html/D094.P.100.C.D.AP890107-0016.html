[1] Emperor Hirohito will be remembered for one overwhelming decision during his 62-year reign. [2] Japan must surrender for the first time in its history and end World War II. [3] Hirohito, who became emperor in 1926 at the age of 25, is generally portrayed as having played no significant role in Japan's military adventures in the 30's and 40's. [4] In 1945, after the U.S. dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, Hirohito spoke out against continuing the war and in a radio address to the nation announced Japan's surrender. [5] After the war, the emperor was mainly a figurehead, but the imperial system has remained important to Japanese.
