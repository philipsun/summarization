[1] New treatments that stop a heart attack in its tracks, may help victims leave the hospital as early as three days after their attacks. [2] Early discharges were made possible by two medical advances, tissue plasminogen activator (TPA) and angioplasty. [3] TPA can dissolve clots in the heart's arteries and minimize damage and angioplasty can clear arteries by opening them with tiny balloons. [4] Hospital stays have been steadily shortening over the past decades as doctors learned that prolonged inactivity caused patients to develop blood clots and become demoralized. [5] If 15% of heart attack patients were discharges after three days, the annual savings would be $280 million.
