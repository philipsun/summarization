[1] The mountainous area of northern Iran was rocked by an earthquake measuring between 7.3 and 7.7 on the Richter scale early Thursday morning. [2] Killing as many as 55,000 people and injuring thousands of others, the earthquake occurred as most people were asleep. [3] The United States will be providing $300,000 in supplies through the American Red Cross and other U.S. agencies. [4] Iran, which broke diplomatic ties with the U.S. in 1979, has welcomed the aid. [5] Russia has begun to airlift supplies into the area and is sending a rescue team from Soviet Armenia, the site of a devastating 1988 earthquake.
