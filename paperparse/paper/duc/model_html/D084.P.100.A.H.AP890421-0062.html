[1] The 16-inch guns on 1942 Iowa-class ships are criticized as archaic, dangerous technology unfit for the nuclear missile age. [2] They are the only guns in the Navy that still use bagged powder -- packed in silk bags and ignited by an electric spark. [3] In newer guns, powder is packed in metal containers. [4] Missiles have integrated propellants. [5] The battleships themselves, ordered out of mothballs and upgraded during the Reagan military buildup, are said to have outlived their purpose. [6] The Navy says ships and guns are still useful. [7] An explosion killed 47 aboard the Iowa on Wednesday while a turret crew loaded gunpowder for target practice.
