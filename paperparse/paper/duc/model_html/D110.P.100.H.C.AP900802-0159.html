[1] An emergency session of the foreign ministers of the Arab League was convened in Cairo at Kuwait's request soon after Iraq's military forces overran the small Persian Gulf nation. [2] Kuwait asked the League to condemn the attack. [3] Only Lebanon joined Kuwait in publicly condemning the action, the first time in modern history one Arab nation has taken over the government of another. [4] Kuwait invoked the 1950 Joint Defense Pact that calls on League members to come to the aid of any member that is a victim of military action. [5] The first session adjourned without taking any action.
