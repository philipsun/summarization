[1] North America's crippling drought could result in food shortages for poor, food- importing countries facing high grain prices. [2] Food aid from developed countries could also fall. [3] World cereal production in 1988 is expected to be 1.785 billion tons, only two million tons more than last year's lower-than-expected production. [4] World grain stocks will likely fall below 310 million tons, or 17% of the level necessary to safeguard world food security. [5] The most severely hit crops are coarse grains (maize, barley, oats). [6] Good harvests in Europe and Asia are projected, but locusts flourishing in unexpectedly heavy rains have damaged sub-Saharan crops. [7] South American grain production should be slightly lower.
