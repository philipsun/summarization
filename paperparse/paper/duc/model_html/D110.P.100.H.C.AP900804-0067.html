[1] Saudi Arabia's reaction to the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait has been subdued. [2] The presence of Iraqi forces near the border concerns the Saudis who do not want to provoke Iraq into attacking Saudi oilfields only 150 miles south of the border. [3] Oil executives cite this as reason for believing Saudi Arabia will not close the pipeline that carries exported Iraqi oil across Saudi Arabia. [4] Saudi Arabia did join the other five members of the Gulf Cooperation Council in low-key condemnation of the invasion. [5] The Council maintains a military force of only 3,000 that is no match for Iraq.
