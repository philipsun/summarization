[1] Iraqi President Saddam Hussein saw the invasion of Kuwait as an opportunity to solve his financial problems and fulfill his territorial ambitions. [2] Iraq's eight-year war with Iran left Iraq with a 70 billion dollar debt that was a drag on the economy. [3] Saddam was incensed that Kuwait demanded repayment of wartime loans. [4] Hussein also accused Kuwait of stealing 2.4 billion dollars worth of oil from a disputed border area, and costing Iraq oil revenue by ignoring production quotas established by OPEC. [5] By controlling Kuwait, Iraq could also fulfill its longstanding wish to for more gulf coastline.
