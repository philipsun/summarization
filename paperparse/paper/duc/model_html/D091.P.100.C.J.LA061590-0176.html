[1] A flash flood Thursday night sent a wall of water 15 to 20 feet high through communities along the Ohio River, killing at least 10 people and leaving 40 to 60 missing, authorities said. [2] The flood occurred after thunderstorms Thursday night dumped about 4 inches of rain in about an hour. [3] Two creeks outside of Shadyside, Ohio overflowed, knocking homes off their foundations and washing away a Shadyside bar filled with people. [4] Gov. Richard Celeste declared a state of emergency. [5] The Shadyside Fire Department established a command center at the Jefferson Elementary School to coordinate rescue efforts.
