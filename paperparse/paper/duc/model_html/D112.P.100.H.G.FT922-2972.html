[1] About 20 banks were concerned last summer at the behavior of businesses run by Robert Maxwell before his empire collapsed. [2] Although concerned about the impact of late payments, the banks did not discuss those concerns with each other. [3] The banks were involved in a flurry of foreign exchange deals in which the Maxwell Communications Corporation (MCC) would buy currency in one financial center while promising to make payment in another center. [4] Several times, MCC received the currency but didn't make the payment, thereby giving themselves an unauthorized loan. [5] Last year, MCC made many repayments to banks by looting pension fund assets.
