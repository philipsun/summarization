[1] State Farm and Nationwide, the two dominant insurers in the Carolinas, expect $600 million in claims from Hurricane Hugo, making it the most expensive storm ever for the insurance industry. [2] Property damage escalated because Hugo scored a direct hit on Charleston and then veered northwest. [3] Overall claims will exceed the record $752.5 million after Hurricane Frederic in 1979. [4] Reinsurers, who share risk in exchange for part of the premiums, and spread their own risk over many companies, will pick up much of the costs. [5] The billions paid out this year because of Hugo and other catastrophes will cause insurance firms to raise prices -- good news for companies and shareholders.
