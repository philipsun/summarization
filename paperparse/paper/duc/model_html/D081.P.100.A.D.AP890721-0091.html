[1] Coal miners in Siberia ended their strike after exacting promises of better food, housing and working conditions, but the wave of unrest they launched continues in other key coal regions. [2] Despite President Gorbachev's announcement of sweeping concessions, hundreds of thousands of miners stayed off the job elsewhere. [3] The labor unrest is the Soviet Union's worst in 60 years. [4] Until Gorbachev rose to power in 1985, strikes were not tolerated. [5] The coal industry employs more than 1 million workers. [6] The government has agreed to make all coal operations economically and legally independent. [7] Gorbachev is trying to end central control of the economy.
