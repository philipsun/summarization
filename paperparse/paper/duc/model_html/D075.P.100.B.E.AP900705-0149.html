[1] According to a study published in the journal Science, cardiac cell damage following a heart attack may be limited by a natural substance called transforming growth factor beta. [2] In studies with rats induced to have heart attacks, those rats receiving injections of TGF beta had 50 percent less cell damage than those not receiving the injections. [3] TGF beta is normally present in heart cells but is missing from rat heart cells damaged by the simulated heart attack. [4] The extent of heart cell damage was determined by measuring the amount of creatine kinase in the heart tissue. [5] Hearts damaged by a heart attack tended to lose creatine kinase.
