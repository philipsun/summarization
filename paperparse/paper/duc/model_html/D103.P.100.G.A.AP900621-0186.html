[1] The earthquake that hit Iran early Thursday exacted particularly large casualties because people were asleep in fragile homes built on a flood plain of loosely deposited soil that shifts in an earthquake. [2] The quake, measuring 7.7 on the Richter scale, hit a mountainous section of coastal Iran at 12:30 a.m. and was the most powerful ever recorded in that area where two major plates of the earth's crust meet. [3] Most structures in the area are built of a ceramic-type brick or adobe that collapses easily. [4] At least 25,000 died and thousands were injured as residents were buried in the rubble of their homes.
