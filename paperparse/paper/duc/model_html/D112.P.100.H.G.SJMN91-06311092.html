[1] Robert Maxwell, the flamboyant millionaire who built a global publishing empire, was found dead yesterday in waters off the Canary Islands where he had been vacationing on his yacht. [2] Maxwell, 68, who earlier this year had bought the New York Daily News, vanished overnight from his yacht. [3] The Czechoslovak-born Maxwell, who escaped the Holocaust and immigrated to Britain in 1940, personally ran his own publishing empire. [4] His sons Kevin and Ian took over the business Tuesday. [5] His death prompted immediate concern in British financial circles about the future of his debt-laden empire and the future of the New York Daily News.
