[1] Iraq's powerful army met little resistance when it invaded Kuwait on 2 August 1990. [2] The invasion came as no surprise. [3] Iraqi President Hussein had accused Kuwait of stealing oil from a border area, and exceeding OPEC production goals. [4] The United Nations Security Council voted on 6 August to impose trade and military sanctions on Iraq. [5] Some major oil importers had already embargoed Iraqi oil. [6] Oil prices soared. [7] The United States moved an aircraft carrier into the Arabian Sea. [8] Iraq warned its people of a possible United States attack. [9] President Bush only said that all options were open.
