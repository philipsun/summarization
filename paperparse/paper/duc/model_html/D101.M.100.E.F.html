[1] Soviet hard-liners staged a coup in order to prevent implementation of Gorbachev's reforms. [2] Some viewers suspected a Gorbachev role in the coup as a ploy to solidify his position. [3] The coup ended after three days due mainly to opposition by Boris Yeltsin who rallied thousands in opposition. [4] Coup leaders were arrested and Gorbachev was returned to the presidency. [5] Gorbachev still hoped to implement reforms working through the Communist Party. [6] Yeltsin, however, had quit the party and wanted to see its influence lessened. [7] Yeltsin was clearly moving into a greater leadership role in the new Russia.
