[1] An estimated 200,000 black gold and coal miners in South Africa are expected to begin a strike for higher wages and better working conditions. [2] It would be the largest job action ever to affect the mining industry in that nation. [3] If other mines join in, a total of 300,000 miners could be on strike. [4] Gold and coal account for more than half of South Africa's foreign-currency holdings. [5] The union is demanding a 30% across-the-board wage increase for black miners who it says earn an average of $109 a month. [6] The Chamber of Mines says they earn twice that much.
