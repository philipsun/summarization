[1] The Oscar, created 60 years ago by MGM art director Cedric Gibbons, weighs 8 pounds and stands 13 inches tall. [2] It is made of Britannia metal and plated with copper, nickel, and gold. [3] From 1942-44 it was made of plaster. [4] It is manufactured by Chicago's R.S. Owens Co. 1,816 have been produced so far. [5] The only engraving mistake was "Dick Tracy" instead of "Spencer Tracy" in 1938. [6] The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences reserves the first right to buy an Oscar from anyone. [7] "All About Eve" is the most-nominated film (14), Ben Hur the most awarded (11), and Walt Disney the most- awarded person (32).
