[1] Residents of Lucille Ball's hometown, Jamestown N.Y., remembered the star on Wednesday, following her death in Los Angeles. [2] She had starred in high school plays and appeared on the vaudeville stage. [3] Although Miss Ball had not been to Jamestown since 1956, she maintained friends and donated money to charities and civic events. [4] After she became ill, the town sent her 150 posters with 10,000 signatures. [5] She was to have been honored on May 20 with an honorary degree from the community college and a street renamed Lucy Lane in the village where she was raised. [6] The honors will now be awarded posthumously.
