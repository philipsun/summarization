[1] On January 16, 1989, Patrick Edward Purdy fired an AK-47 automatic assault rifle into a crowded playground at Cleveland Elementary School in Stockton, California, killing five children and wounding 30, then killing himself. [2] All the dead were children of Southeast Asian refugees, as were 60% of the school's students. [3] Translators and Buddhist priests assisted families, and returning students received counseling. [4] A South Carolina school where a similar attack occurred sent encouragement. [5] Governor Deukmejian spoke at one memorial service. [6] Anti-racism statements were made.
