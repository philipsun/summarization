[1] Workers at KGHM in Lubin, Poland's copper producing combine went on strike yesterday as coal-mining unions staged token stoppages in a third of the country's pits in a major challenge to the government's economic policies. [2] The KGHM strike came despite a management offer to increase wages by 20%. [3] Coal mining unions are also demanding wage increases as well as a clear restructuring strategy for their industry. [4] The copper industry continues to show profit but coal mining is running at a loss. [5] This has led to temporary lay-offs, which hit miners' incomes and led to the present bout of unrest.
