[1] Ousted East German leader Erich Honecker was arrested Monday morning morning upon release from hospital and taken to prison. [2] Honecker, who expected to be indicted for high treason, was immediately taken to East Berlin's Rummelsburg prison. [3] He had previously been declared to ill to withstand imprisonment. [4] Honecker, the aging hard-line Stalinist who ruled East Germany for 18 years, had recently undergone surgery for a malignant kidney tumor. [5] Earlier this month, East German prosecutors said Honecker and former state security chief Erich Mielke would be charged with treason and corruption for misuse of their positions and state funds.
