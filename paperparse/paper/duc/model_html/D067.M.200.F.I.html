[1] Damage from Hurricane Andrew is estimated at $20 billion, with only $8 billion covered by insurance. [2] The last serious hurricane caused $4.2 billion in insured loses in 1989. [3] American insurers most affected by claims from Andrew are the State Farm Group, Allstate, Hartford Insurance, Aetna, and Travellers. [4] General Accident, the leading British insurer, expects their claims to rise as high as $40 million, which means the company may be able to pass losses on to external reinsurers. [5] Andrew hit the Bahamas, killing two, then on August 24 tore through southern Florida, where 15 were killed and power outages and polluted water plagued the residents for several days. [6] The city of Homestead and the nearby air force base were flattened. [7] In Dade County, 250,000 people are without homes. [8] After gathering strength over the Gulf of Mexico, Andrew next smashed into Louisiana, killing two and injuring dozens. [9] It did miss the important oil refineries, but damaged crops and the oyster and alligator businesses. [10] Andrew was downgraded to a tropical storm as it moved inland. [11] While Andrew ravaged the land, the dollar sustained a similar assault. [12] Bond yields rose as the Dow Jones and the dollar fell. [13] Insurance stock also fell, but home construction stock had big gains.
