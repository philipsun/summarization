[1] Senator Bob Dole of Kansas made the nominating speech for Senator Dan Quayle for vice president at the Republican Convention. [2] In the speech, Dole called Quayle a dedicated public servant and a devoted family man. [3] Dole said that Dan was always willing to make the tough choices and cast the tough votes. [4] He was also characterized as an advocate and supporter of the Reagan revolution working to transform Reagan's vision into the law. [5] Dole also cited what he characterized as negative aspects of Democratic presidential candidate Michael Dukakis' experience. [6] Dole then cited Quayle's twelve years experience tackling national and international problems.
