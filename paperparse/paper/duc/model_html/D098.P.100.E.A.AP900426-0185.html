[1] Six hours after a failed attempt, ground controllers re-established contact Thursday with the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) through its main antennas. [2] Controllers sent positional information to the telescope and widened the radio beam to cover a larger area around the two high-speed antennas. [3] Space shuttle Discovery trailed 57 miles behind the HST and was to stay nearby until the Hubble's aperture door is opened Friday morning. [4] If signals fail to open the door, astronauts will walk into space and crank it open Saturday. [5] The shuttle, scheduled to return to earth on Sunday, would extend its mission if the space walk is necessary.
