[1] An overcrowded ferry without life jackets capsized Saturday morning in the monsoon- swollen Ganges River. [2] The two-decker boat was in 12-30 feet of water 100 yards from shore. [3] 400 died, including 300 on the lower deck where women and children rode. [4] Five hundred thirty-five passengers were aboard the 35-year-old steam-powered ferry designed to carry 160. [5] Passengers protested when 150 wheat bags were loaded too. [6] Strong currents and poor visibility hampered rescue efforts. [7] The trip is used by Hindu pilgrims crossing the river to a shrine. [8] This was India's worst ferry accident ever. [9] In July 1970, 150 died when a boat capsized in the Krishna River.
