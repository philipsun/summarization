[1] Lucille Ball, the pioneering queen of television comedy, died Wednesday at 77 of a ruptured abdominal artery. [2] She had been recovering steadily from heart surgery at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center when the aorta burst, sending her into cardiac arrest. [3] Accolades immediately poured in from friends, former co-workers, and Presidents. [4] She and her first husband, Desi Arnaz, produced and co-starred on the precedence setting "I Love Lucy", one of the most popular TV shows ever. [5] She was born in New York, became a model, then appeared in 75 plus movies. [6] In recent years her career slowed and she last performed at the March Academy Awards.
