[1] Lucille Ball was born August 6, 1911, in Jamestown, New York. [2] She began, in 1926, as a chorus girl and moved to Hollywood in 1933, appearing in over 75 films. [3] She married Desi Arnaz in 1940 and, in 1951, they formed Desilu Productions and began "I Love Lucy". [4] When Lucy and Desi divorced in 1960, Lucy bought Desi's share of the company and sold it in 1967 for $18 million. [5] In 1961, she married Gary Morton and next year started "The Lucy Show", which became "Here's Lucy" and ended in 1974. [6] She later starred in two movies and another, short-lived, TV show.
