[1] The Seoul, Korea 1988 Summer Olympics closed on Sunday. [2] 9,633 athletes from 160 nations competed in 34 venues. [3] At least 1 billion people watched via a 115-nation worldwide TV hookup. [4] It was the first meeting between the Soviet Union and the United States since 1976. [5] The Soviets captured the most medals, the U.S. third most. [6] Among the U.S. highlights were championships won by Greg Louganis, Florence Griffith Joyner, Jackie Joyner-Kersee and Matt Biondi. [7] A big Olympic negative was the stripping of the gold medal from Canadian sprinter Ben Johnson because of drug use. [8] He flew home in disgrace.
