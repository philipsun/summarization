[1] In mid-July, 1988's drought looked record-breaking. [2] Further drought was predicted based on past records. [3] Severe storms brought tornadoes, hail damage, and lightning-sparked fires. [4] States issued heat alerts. [5] Weather experts who attended a Drought Symposium urged continued attention to climate and greenhouse-effect reduction. [6] Crop declines were forecast and grain futures fell. [7] Adequate domestic and export supplies were expected. [8] UPS, railroads, and truckers transported hay for free. [9] Federal aid, state loans to farmers, and eased future crop controls were proposed. [10] Reagan visited drought areas. [11] Farmers sued reneging insurers. [12] Drinking water was hauled in and dam flows reduced. [13] Livestock sales increased. [14] Cannery, mill, and logging jobs fell. [15] Highway projects were accelerated.
