[1] Relatives of the 47 victims killed in the No.2 gun explosion aboard the Iowa last Wednesday were among the 6000 people who attended a memorial at Norfolk Naval Air Station on Monday. [2] President Bush, a WWII Navy pilot, and the ship's commander, Capt. Fred P. Moosally, led the service. [3] Moosally praised the No. 2 gun turret crew as the best on the ship. [4] At the end of the service, President and Mrs. Bush greeted and consoled the families of the lost men. [5] As many as 500 Iowa crewmen, delayed by traffic, were unable to attend because of presidential security measures.
