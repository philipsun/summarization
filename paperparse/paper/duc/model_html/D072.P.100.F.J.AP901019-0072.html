[1] During his 47-year association with the Philharmonic Symphony Orchestra, Leonard Bernstein led 1244 concerts. [2] Tonight the Philharmonic commemorated their laureate conductor who died four days ago at age 72. [3] Leonard Slatkin directed an extraordinary all Bernstein program. [4] The somber mood in the hall was initially broken by the overture to "Candide" (1956) and "Serenade for Solo Violin, String Orchestra, Harp and Percussion" (1954). [5] The mourning returned, however, with "Jeremiah" (1942). [6] During it, mezzo-soprano Wendy White, as well as audience members, shed tears. [7] The final selection, "Chichester Psalms" (1965) ended with a chorus declaring a message Bernstein sought for society: "Together in unity".
