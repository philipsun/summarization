[1] The Senate panel weighing John Tower's nomination for defense secretary has found a substantial discrepancy in his description of his business dealings with British Aerospace. [2] He told the committee that he represented that company on civil aviation matters and not on military systems. [3] But in a divorce deposition in 1986, he said that he represented that firm on a range of military problems. [4] This discrepancy prompts concerns not only about his veracity but also whether he should have registered as a lobbyist under the Foreign Agents Registration Act. [5] The FBI is looking into new charges about Tower's alleged drinking, womanizing, and receipt of campaign contributions.
