[1] Supercomputers and satellites helped predict the path and landfall of Hurricane Hugo in time to allow evacuation of Charleston, S.C., but hurricane tracking is not an exact science. [2] The intensity of Hugo was upgraded on the basis of Air Force reconnaissance just hours before it hit Charleston. [3] There are no reliable computer models for forecasting strength. [4] To predict the path, supercomputers use satellite data to make constantly changing predictions. [5] Forecasters analyze the predictions, satellite data, the history of similar storms and the current path to make an educated guess about the landfall. [6] The best information for predicting hurricanes still comes from flying airplanes into the hurricane.
