[1] The exact distance of a marathon is 26 miles and 385 yards. [2] It is often expressed as 26.2 miles. [3] Marathons are very popular, and major cities of the world organize marathons that draw thousands of participants. [4] London, Berlin, Rome, Paris, Stockholm, Vienna, Budapest, Brussels, Munich, Bangkok and Tel Aviv are some of the cities that organize annual marathons. [5] Tour companies even offer "marathon tours" to distant marathons. [6] Most of the thousands who run do so only for self-satisfaction, but others compete for monetary prizes offered by most marathons. [7] The Marine Corps marathon in Washington, D.C. is one that does not offer prize money. [8] Marathons are usually run in the early morning hours to take advantage of cooler temperatures and cleaner air. [9] The organizers of the 1992 Barcelona Olympics were criticized for contesting the marathon in the late afternoon heat. [10] The London Marathon is the world's largest. [11] The Boston Marathon is the most storied. [12] It goes back almost a century, and in 1992, 83 year-old John Kelley was planning to run in his 60th Boston Marathon. [13] The Los Angeles Marathon is renown as the best-marketed marathon. [14] An Diego and San Francisco pride themselves for providing the most marathon-friendly weather.
