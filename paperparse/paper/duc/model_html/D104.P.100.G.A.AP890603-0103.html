[1] Chinese troops stormed Tiananmen Square early Sunday, shooting and beating their way through crowds of pro-democracy protesters. [2] The assault came two weeks after Premier Li Peng declared martial law in Beijing. [3] Sporadic clashes were reported on Saturday as soldiers made several attempts to reach the square. [4] They retreated temporarily under a barrage of rocks and other objects but resumed their push more violently at about 8 p.m. firing weapons and using tear gas as they moved toward Tiananmen. [5] By 3:30 a.m. Sunday troops had surrounded the square. [6] At 4:30 armored personnel carriers entered the square and began slowly moving toward the several thousand students who remained.
