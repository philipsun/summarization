[1] The Discovery crew deployed the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) on Wednesday but stayed nearby in case there was trouble opening the aperture door. [2] The door was opened Friday morning, exposing the telescope's finely polished 95.5-inch eye to starlight for the first time. [3] When the door opened, however, two of HST's four position- stabilizing gyroscopes stopped working. [4] Engineers got the gyroscopes back on line, but there were still problems with linking the high-speed dish antennas with a relay satellite so the planned release on Tuesday of HST's first test image has been delayed. [5] Scientific data will follow in a month or two.
