[1] The Senate Foreign Relations Committee endorsed the nomination of Republican activist Peter Secchia to be ambassador to Italy. [2] The chairman, Senator Pell, had many misgivings about the nomination but did not vote against Secchia. [3] Secchia pledged to be more careful in his use of language as a diplomat that he had used as an activist. [4] Pell was not convinced. [5] He said that the nomination was a mistake and that "in all likelihood Mr. Secchia will be an embarrassment to the administration and the United States". [6] However, since Secchia is the president's choice he would not try to block it.
