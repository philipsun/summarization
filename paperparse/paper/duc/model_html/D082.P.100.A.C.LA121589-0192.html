[1] Andrei Sakharov, the 68 year-old scientist and human rights activist, died Thursday of an apparent heart attack in his Moscow apartment. [2] Colleagues in the Soviet Congress observed a minute of silence in honor of the man who was considered a national hero for his work on the hydrogen bomb, and later reviled for his stand on human rights. [3] President Gorbachev praised him as a "man of conviction and sincerity". [4] Tributes poured in from around the world. [5] President Bush, Prime Minister Thatcher and Lech Walesa were among those honoring the winner of the Nobel Peace Prize.
