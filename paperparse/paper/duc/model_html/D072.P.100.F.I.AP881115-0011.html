[1] Leonard Bernstein returned to Carnegie Hall with the New York Philharmonic on Monday, exactly 45 years after his triumphant conducting debut. [2] He first conducted here as an emergency fill-in with no rehearsal. [3] That concert was nationally broadcast and catapulted Bernstein to fame at age 25. [4] The shimmering performance, the 1,215th time he conducted this orchestra, consisted of three Bernstein compositions--"Chichester Psalms", a serenade, and a symphony. [5] Performers from his first concert attended on Monday night, including a violinist who played on both occasions. [6] Bernstein had celebrated his 70th birthday this summer at Tanglewood and asked on Monday, "Will this birthday never end"?
