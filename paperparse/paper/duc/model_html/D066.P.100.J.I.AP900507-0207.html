[1] Wal-Mart Stores Inc.'s Visitors Center opens Wednesday, 40 years after Sam Walton opened a business in the same building in Bentonville, Arkansas. [2] The center chronicles the rise of the nation's fastest-growing discount chain. [3] After working for the J.C. Penney Co. and a tour in the army, Walton opened a Ben Franklin Store in nearby Newport. [4] He moved to Bentonville in 1950. [5] He opened more Ben Franklin Stores, but the company did not like his plan for discount stores in rural areas. [6] Walton and his brother opened the first Wal-Mart Discount City in 1962. [7] By early 1990 Wal-Mart owned 1,402 stores and employed 272,000.
