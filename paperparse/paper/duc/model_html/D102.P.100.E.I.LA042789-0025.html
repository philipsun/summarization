[1] Lucille Ball, 77, died early Wednesday while recovering from heart surgery. [2] She was the queen of television comedy. [3] She and her husband Desi Arnaz created "I Love Lucy" with its weekly wackiness watched by 40 million. [4] Born in upstate New York, she was a chorus girl and model before Hollywood. [5] "I Love Lucy" was the first TV comedy to be filmed, leaving a legacy of reruns. [6] After her divorce from Arnaz, Miss Ball married Gary Morton and starred in other shows. [7] "Here's Lucy" included her two children. [8] In later life she appeared in several movies and garnered awards and tributes.
