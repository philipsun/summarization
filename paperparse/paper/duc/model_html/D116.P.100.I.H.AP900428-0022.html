[1] The shuttle that put the Hubble Space Telescope into orbit carried the eyepiece from Mount Wilson Observatory's 100-inch Hooker Telescope, constructed in 1918. [2] It was the world's most powerful telescope until 1948, when the 200-inch Hale Telescope went into operation at Mount Palomar Observatory. [3] Astronomer Edwin Hubble used the Hooker in the 1920s and 1930s to take thousands of photographs of faint galaxies. [4] He provided the first definite proof that other galaxies existed and observational evidence for Einstein's theory that the universe was expanding. [5] He helped convince scientists the universe was created in a giant explosion 15 billion years ago. [6] The Hooker Telescope was mothballed in 1985.
