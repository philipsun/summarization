[1] Robert Maxwell was described as larger than life and the Citizen Kane of his time. [2] Born in Czechoslovakia, Maxwell escape the Holocaust and emigrated to Britain in 1940. [3] He put together and personally ran a publishing empire which included Macmillan publishing house in New York; Britain's Daily Mirror, The Daily Record, and the European and British Printing Company; and Communications Corp., Britain's largest printing company. [4] Details of Maxwell's death are sketchy and the cause of his death is not known although it is speculated that he slipped and fell overboard. [5] Maxwell's death came as he was embroiled in an international financial controversy.
