[1] A drug, diltiazen, used to treat patients complaining of chest pain could help prevent heart attacks in four out five patients. [2] But, diltiazen increases the subsequent heart attack in patients whose hearts pump abnormally. [3] Study results suggest the drug may be effective in decreasing the likelihood of a second heart attack in the majority of patients. [4] But patients with pulmonary congestion given diltiazen, increase their chances of a subsequent heart attack. [5] Diliazen, a calcium entry blocker, is one of a new class of drugs in the fight against heart disease. [6] Nitrates and beta-blockers are the other common drugs.
