[1] Democrats said three of Bush's ambassadorial nominees -- Florida real estate developers Joseph Zappala and Marvin Sembler to Spain and Australia, respectively; and Seattle real estate broker Della Newman to New Zealand -- had no qualifications except political connections. [2] Senator Paul Sarbanes led the opposition, saying the appointments were prompted by campaign contributions. [3] Zappala's only government experience was chairing Republican campaign finance committees. [4] The Senate Foreign Relations Committee narrowly approved the nominations Tuesday. [5] Ambassadorial nominations approved routinely were Shirley Temple Black to Czechoslovakia, Thomas Melady to the Vatican, Keith Brown to Denmark, and Joseph Gildenhorn to Switzerland. [6] William Howard Taft IV was approved as permanent US representative to NATO.
