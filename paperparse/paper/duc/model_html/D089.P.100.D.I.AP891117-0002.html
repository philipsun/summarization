[1] Federal aid was approved Friday for tornado-ravaged Huntsville, Alabama. [2] Governor Guy Hunt viewed the destruction, toured hospitals, and visited with grieving families. [3] Seventeen were killed, 400 injured, and 80 are still in hospitals. [4] State emergency officials estimate damages in excess of $100 million. [5] About $1 million will be needed for grants to pay for emergency clothing and temporary housing. [6] The Federal Emergency Management Agency hopes to begin taking relief applications on Sunday. [7] Food has arrived in the city, but there no longer is a place to store it. [8] Flags will be flown at half- staff through Tuesday in memory of the victims.
