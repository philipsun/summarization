[1] Relatives of the 47 victims killed in the explosion aboard the Iowa last Wednesday were among the 6000 people who attended a memorial at Norfolk Naval Air Station on Monday. [2] President Bush and Iowa commander Capt. Moosally attended the service. [3] The USS Iowa, a WW II battleship returned to duty in 1982, was conducting firing exercises northeast of Puerto Rico when the blast occurred in the No. 2 gun turret. [4] The Defense Department said it possibly originated from a 110-pound gunpowder bag used to fire shells weighing over a ton a distance of 23 miles. [5] Eleven people inside the turret, all on the lowest deck loading powder, survived the Navy said. [6] The gun had not been fired. [7] Two of the 11 survivors stood atop the fire-blackened turret when the Iowa docked at Norfolk Naval Base Sunday. [8] The Navy released a videotape of the explosion made by an officer of the Iowa who was recording the firing of the big guns, but it won't speculate on the cause of the tragedy. [9] The Navy is investigating the causes of the accident and the Senate Armed Services Committee will hold hearings. [10] The Pentagon ordered a moratorium on firings of the 16-inch guns.
