[1] The Indian government imposed direct federal rule in Kashmir where their security forces are battling to put down rebellion by Moslem separatists. [2] This action follows the killing of at least 29 Moslem militants by Indian security forces. [3] Meanwhile, in the Pakistani capital Islamabad, Pakistan and India began negotiations to repair deteriorating conditions between the two countries. [4] India claims, and Pakistan denies, that Kashmiri militants cross into Pakistan for arms and training. [5] The announcement of federal rule was made in the belief that the state was too unstable for elected government. [6] Since India cracked down on the militants, 792 people have been killed.
