[1] In the recent San Francisco earthquake, why did some luxury four-story apartments or office buildings collapse while high-rises escaped virtually unscathed? [2] Accordingly, some buildings collapsed because of two things: very poor soil and very poor structural design. [3] Some were built over landfill and others were erected earlier when there were no earthquake codes. [4] Four-story buildings with garages underneath have no walls on one side. [5] Connections break and the front falls. [6] Skyscrapers usually survive because architects factor in wind gusts and earth movement. [7] In smaller buildings, contractors make sure that vertical forces--such as snow and ice--cannot force a collapse.
