[1] On August 2nd, 1990, Iraq occupied Kuwait with 100,000 troops, destroying government buildings and installations, killing 200, and replacing fleeing Kuwaitis with Iraqis. [2] Saddam Hussain accused Kuwait of overproducing to drive down oil prices and stealing oil from Iraqi border wells. [3] Iraq claimed it invaded at revolutionaries' request. [4] Kuwait said Iraq invaded because Kuwait pressed for war loan repayment. [5] Kuwait appealed for US and UN help. [6] Bush pledged to defend its Persian Gulf interests and sent a naval group. [7] The US, UN, and EEC imposed widely-supported trade and military sanctions against Iraq and Kuwait. [8] Iraq threatened to hold citizens of sanction-imposing countries hostage. [9] Oil prices soared.
