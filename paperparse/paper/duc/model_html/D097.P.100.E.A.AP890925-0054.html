[1] Local authorities credit residents who showed respect for hurricane warnings for the low casualty rate from Hurricane Hugo. [2] Charleston S.C. bore the brunt of Hugo's fury, but only two of the state's 17 deaths were in the Charleston area. [3] A local Red Cross official estimated that 18,000 residents of Charleston and surrounding counties headed for shelters and tens of thousands motored inland. [4] Charleston Mayor Riley said that those in shelters and those who evacuated inland "...were potential victims of injury and death". [5] North Charleston Mayor Bourne credited people for heeding the warnings, saying "It was bad, but it could have been a lot worse".
