[1] Deaths from heart and blood vessel disease dropped 24% over the past decade. [2] But it remains by far the nation's largest killer. [3] Experts attribute the decline to a combination of healthier living habits, including fewer cigarettes, better food and improved medical treatment. [4] Statistics show that more than one in four Americans suffer from some form of cardiovascular disease and half of these will eventually die from it. [5] Since 60% of heart attacks occur before the victim reaches the hospital, a major goal is getting people to go to the hospital at the first sign of a heart attack.
