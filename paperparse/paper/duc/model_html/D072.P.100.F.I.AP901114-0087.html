[1] Leonard Bernstein was remembered on Wednesday by a concert on the stage where he made his conducting debut 47 years ago. [2] The Carnegie Hall concert included selections from Bernstein's "Mass" and "Chichester Psalms". [3] The overture of "Candide" was played with a symbolically empty podium. [4] Speakers included his three children, son-in- law, and friends, Lauren Bacall and Schuyler Chapin. [5] Performers included Mstislav Rostropovich, Marilyn Horne, Christa Ludwig, Jerry Hadley, and Chester Ludgin. [6] The first chair musicians from orchestras around the world took part. [7] Wednesday also was the 90th birthday of Bernstein's close friend, Aaron Copland, and the 36th anniversary of Bernstein's first TV broadcast.
