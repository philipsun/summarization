[1] It was announced Thursday that Kazuo Ishiguro won the Booker Prize for his novel "The Remains of The Day". [2] The prize is worth 32,000 dollars. [3] The winning novel is a love story about a butler's English vacation that explores the provoking themes of class, tradition and duty. [4] Ishiguro was born in Japan, and came to England at the age of six He lives in London. [5] He was a Booker finalist in 1986. [6] In accepting the award, Ishiguro paid tribute to Salman Rushdie, the 1988 runner-up for the Booker, who is now in hiding.
