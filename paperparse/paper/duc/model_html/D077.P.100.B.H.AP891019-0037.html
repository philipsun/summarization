[1] Offers of equipment and personnel for San Francisco Bay earthquake survivor searches came from Los Angeles, Los Alamos, Israel, and France. [2] LA shipped doctors, police, and fire investigators. [3] British engineers came to assess structural damage. [4] Charleston sent drinking water, symbolic of support following Hugo. [5] The Salvation Army dispatched 12 mobile canteens. [6] The Red Cross, strained by Hurricane Hugo, opened 25 shelters. [7] Its LA, San Diego and Portland offices sent blood. [8] Osaka, Japan promised $10,000. [9] Anheuser-Busch sent canned water. [10] The Coast Guard sent boats and helicopters. [11] The IRS waived late-filing penalties. [12] President Bush planned to visit and promised $273 million victim aid. [13] San Francisco estimated $2 billion damage.
