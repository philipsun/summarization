[1] Over the weekend Russia dispatched 3,000 special troops to halt ethnic fighting in the troubled Caucasus Mountains. [2] Inguish rebels in North Ossetia were reported to have taken hostage 80 Interior Ministry soldiers based in the region. [3] The Inguish, a regional ethnic group, are demanding return of lands that Stalin expropriated and declared part of North Ossetia. [4] This was the second recent troop deployment. [5] Last week soldiers were sent to help crush a coup attempt in Tajikistan. [6] Several other ethnic conflicts are continuing in republics of the former Soviet Union that are located in the Caucasus Mountains.
