[1] The eyepiece used by astronomer Edwin Hubble from the Mount Wilson Observatory's 100-inch Hooker Telescope, once the world's most powerful, was carried into orbit by the shuttle that launched the space telescope honoring him, a symbolic gesture from astronomy's past to its future. [2] Using the Hooker telescope, Hubble was the first to determine the distance to a galaxy outside the Milky Way. [3] This provided evidence for the concept that the universe is expanding. [4] His work helped convince scientists that the universe was created in a giant explosion, called the "big bang". [5] The Hooker Telescope was mothballed in 1985.
