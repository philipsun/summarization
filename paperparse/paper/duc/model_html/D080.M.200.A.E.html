[1] The McMartin Pre-School child molestation case, the longest and costliest criminal trial in U.S. history ended with the acquittal of Raymond Buckey and his mother, Peggy McMartin Buckey, on 52 charges. [2] A decision has yet to be reached on whether to retry Buckey on the 13 charges on which the jury deadlocked. [3] The case originally involved Buckey and his mother, as well as his sister and grandmother and three other employees of the school. [4] The charges against the sister, grandmother and the three employees were dropped for lack of evidence. [5] In reaching their verdict, the jury said that the videotaped interviews of the child were a factor in finding the Buckeys innocent. [6] The jurors felt the interviewers asked leading questions and never allowed the children to tell their own story. [7] A number of the jurors stated that they believed some of the children had been molested but that the prosecution had not proven that Raymond Buckey was responsible. [8] The verdict was greeted with anger by the parents of the children involved who felt the prosecution and the legal system had let them down. [9] The judge in the case remarked that the case had poisoned everyone it touched.
