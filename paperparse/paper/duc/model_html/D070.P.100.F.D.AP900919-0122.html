[1] East Germany's ousted secret police chief, Erich Mielke, threatened to break former Communist leader Erich Honecker's neck when the two faced each other in jail. [2] After the popular revolt last fall that ousted the Communist regime and opened the way for German unification, Mielke and other former Communist leaders were jailed. [3] Mielke remains in custody, Honecker was released for health reasons. [4] Many of the prison guards had worked for Mielke's secret police and might interpret his threat against Honecker as an order to kill him. [5] Honecker will be moved to a West Berlin prison after unification if his health improves.
