[1] The Xiangijiang River, swollen by rainstorms to a record level of 38.93 m, has flooded China's Hunan province capital city, Changsha. [2] Yesterday six villages and one fishing ground at Yulushan township were flooded. [3] Nineteen dykes in the Changsha area have collapsed. [4] The Changsha city government asked all flood threatened residents to evacuate. [5] By early today, roads and bridge surfaces in Changsha's Wangyuehu district and some city streets were inundated. [6] Over 500,000 soldiers and local residents are helping to fight the floods. [7] The Changsha city committee of the Communist Party urges the people to unite as one in battling the floods.
