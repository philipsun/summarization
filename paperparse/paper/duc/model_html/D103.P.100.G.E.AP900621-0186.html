[1] The early morning earthquake which struck Iran killed at least 25,000 and injured thousands of others as they slept on Thursday. [2] Scores of villages were destroyed in this mountainous region of coastal Iran, burying the inhabitants. [3] The fragile homes, built on a flood plain of loose soil, were destroyed when the soil shifted during the earthquake, allowing the structures to collapse. [4] The 7.7 magnitude earthquake is the largest ever recorded in the area where two major plates of the earth meet. [5] The earthquake is very similar to one which struck Soviet Armenia on Dec. 7, 1988, and killed 25,000 people.
