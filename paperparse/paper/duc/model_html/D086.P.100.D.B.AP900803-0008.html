[1] The events between Iraq and Kuwait from July 17 through August 2 resulted in war. [2] Saddam Hussein accused Kuwait of driving down oil prices by overproduction thereby costing Iraq billions in lost revenues, of stealing Iraqi oil from a common field along their border, and of conspiring with the U.S. and Israel to weaken Iraq. [3] Kuwait suggested that Iraq's belligerence was to force Kuwait to forgive billions of Iraqi war debt. [4] Kuwait accepted an OPEC plan to halt overproduction of oil. [5] Iraq then amassed troops on Kuwait's border, pulled out of talks being held in Saudi Arabia with Kuwait to resolve issues and attacked.
