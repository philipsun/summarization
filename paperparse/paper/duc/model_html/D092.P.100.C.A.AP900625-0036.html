[1] Planes loaded with earthquake relief supplies are arriving in Iran from around the world, including one from the United States, the first such American benevolence toward Iran since the hostage crisis of 1979-80. [2] Another plane carrying U.S. government aid was due today. [3] Tehran radio said Sunday that 68 relief flights had landed at Tehran's Mehrabad airport during a 24-hour period. [4] Countries sending assistance to victims of Thursday's quake include the Soviet Union, Mexico, the Vatican, West Germany, Turkey, Spain, India, Austria and France. [5] In Lebanon the Iranian-backed Hezbollah said its members were donating 10 percent of their salaries (about $500,000) to help the victims.
