[1] The earthquake that struck Iran reminded a US geophysicist of the 1988 quake in Armenia. [2] The Iran quake was of magnitude 7.7 while the Armenian was 6.9 but both killed about 25,000 persons and for essentially the same reasons: most victims were in houses that were shoddily built or otherwise fragile and could collapse easily. [3] The death toll in Iran was high because it happened at night when most people were asleep. [4] This quake was the strongest ever recorded in Iran, which is in a very active seismic region and has recorded 12 quakes of magnitude 7 in the past 30 years.
