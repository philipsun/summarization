[1] As of Tuesday, veteran US diplomat Ronald Spiers will be the new undersecretary- general for General Assembly affairs, the top-ranking American post at the UN. [2] His nomination had been an open secret for months. [3] He replaces Joseph Verner Reed. [4] Spiers, 64, has served at the US embassy in London and has been ambassador to the Bahamas, Turkey, and Pakistan. [5] He has been a member of the US delegation to the UN and director of political affairs for the US Arms Control and Disarmament Agency. [6] At the State Department, he was director of NATO Affairs, assistant secretary in charge of Political- Military Affairs, director of Intelligence and Research, and undersecretary for management.
