[1] John Major was elected leader of the Conservative Party today. [2] Endorsed by Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher as her successor and heir, Major will become prime minister. [3] At 47, he will be the youngest prime minister since the 19th century. [4] In a secret ballot election among Tory members of parliament, Major won 185 votes, two short of the majority needed to win. [5] His opponents, former Defense Secretary Michael Heseltine with 131 votes and Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd with 56, conceded the election to Major, making a second ballot unnecessary. [6] Major emerged as the possible heir apparent to Thatcher when she appointed him chancellor of the Exchequer in 1989.
