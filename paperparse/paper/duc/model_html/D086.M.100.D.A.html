[1] In July 1990 Saddam Hussein accused Kuwait of violating OPEC production quotas and stealing oil from wells along the Iraq-Kuwait border. [2] The two countries agreed to talks to settle their differences, but on the first day of the talks the Iraqi representatives walked out. [3] The next day, August 2, 1990, 100,000 Iraqi troops invaded and quickly occupied Kuwait. [4] Despite strong diplomatic efforts, Iraq remained defiant, rounding up several hundred Britons, Americans and West Germans in Kuwait and taking some of them to Iraq. [5] As of August 6, 1990 Iraqi troops were massed on the border of Saudi Arabia and the U.S. warned that its vital interests were involved.
