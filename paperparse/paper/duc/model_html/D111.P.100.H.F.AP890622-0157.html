[1] An American oceanographer, Robert Ballard, has discovered the location of the German battleship Bismark, sunk in 1941. [2] Ballard believes the crew scuttled the ship rather than allowing the British to capture or sink it. [3] Structural evidence and the ship sitting upright convinced Ballard of the scuttling rather than the British sinking it. [4] An ocean-crawling robot discovered the ship in deep water about 600 miles west of Brest, France. [5] Ballard said he would provide the exact location only to the Germans so they could decide what, if anything, to do about it. [6] In WWII, Prime Minister Churchill had made sinking the Bismark a priority.
