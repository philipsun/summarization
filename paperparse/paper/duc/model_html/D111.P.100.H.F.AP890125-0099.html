[1] The mysterious sinking of the Lucona in the Indian Ocean ignited a scandal that led to high level political resignations twelve years later. [2] Six sailors died in the sinking, which was believed deliberate and the wrongdoing of Udo Proksch. [3] The ship's cargo was listed as material for a uranium processing plant and was insured for $16 million dollars. [4] The insurance company refused to pay, alleging that the cargo was really scrap metal. [5] Proksch, wanted by Austrian authorities, has fled.
