[1] Sam Walton relied on his personality, hard work and a willingness to adopt new business practices to build Wal-Mart into the country's largest discount retailer. [2] This made him the country's richest man, but he continued to live a simple life. [3] Identification as the richest man made him uncomfortable. [4] He was criticized for anti-union policies and causing the failure of small town businesses where he located his stores. [5] Walton often visited his workers and kept them happy. [6] Shortly before his death in 1992 Walton wrote a folksy autobiography. [7] There is also a "scholarly study" on his business.
