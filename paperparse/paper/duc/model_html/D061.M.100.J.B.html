[1] Tropical Storm Gilbert strengthened into a hurricane on Saturday night, September 10th in the eastern Caribbean. [2] It tracked westerly at about 15mph while building in intensity. [3] After skirting southern Puerto Rico, Haiti and the Dominican Republic, it hit Jamaica with high winds and torrential rains, destroying 100,000 of the countries 500,000 homes and taking nineteen lives. [4] It then passed over the Cayman Islands before slamming into the Yucatan Peninsula causing heavy damage in the Cancun and Cozumel regions. [5] Gilbert is the most intense hurricane ever recorded with a record low barometric pressure of 26.31 inches and sustained winds of 179mph, gusting to 218mph.
