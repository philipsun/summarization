[1] Twenty-three sailors from the Star of Alexandria, a 652 foot freighter, were rescued Monday evening 400 miles southeast of Cape Cod. [2] Two other crew members were missing. [3] The ship could not be spotted, and apparently had gone down in rough seas. [4] The sailors in a lifeboat used a radio dropped by a Coast Guard aircraft to contact the merchant ship Ravenscraig that rescued them. [5] The Star of Alexandria was built in 1966, and was registered in England. [6] It was designed to carry bulk freight, and was carrying cement from Athens to New York when it went down.
