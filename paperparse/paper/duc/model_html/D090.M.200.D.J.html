[1] President Corazon Aquino used military firepower to end the military led rebellion on Mindanao. [2] The Oct. 4-6 uprising never spread to other islands. [3] Aquino has survived two major coup attempts and several military mutinies. [4] The current rebellion is the latest of several military mutinies. [5] In August Aquino reversed a long-held policy and said she was willing to negotiate cease-fires with Communist guerillas and former soldiers who have staged coup attempts. [6] In December 1989 a confrontation between pro-coup supporters and Aquino supporters occurred at the Philippine consulate in Los Angeles. [7] Aquino's government has also had its political controversies. [8] Her opposition to the nomination of Secretary-designate Abad raised doubts about her commitment to social reform. [9] In 1989, her Vice President refused to condemn the insurrection that was then unfolding. [10] Aquino rose to power in 1986 after dictator Ferdinand Marcos, accused of stealing billions of government funds, was ousted. [11] June1988 news stories quoted sources saying Aquino would allow Marcos to return to the Philippines to stand trial, but a year later she stated the critically ill Marcos could not return to die, nor could he be buried in the Philippines. [12] Aquino traveled to West German in 1989 seeking economic assistance.
