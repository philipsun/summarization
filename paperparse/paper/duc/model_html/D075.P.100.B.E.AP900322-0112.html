[1] Danish studies of the drug Verapamil, a calcium channel blocker, show that the drug reduces the chance of a new heart attack in patients recovering from a heart attack. [2] This is the first trial to indicate that calcium blockers may be helpful after a heart attack. [3] Verapamil is about as effective as beta blockers in protecting people after heart attacks and may be useful in instances where beta blockers cannot be used. [4] Many people do not tolerate beate blockers, especially those suffering from asthma and emphysema. [5] Verapamil was effective in decreasing mortality in the first 12 to 18 months following a heart attack.
