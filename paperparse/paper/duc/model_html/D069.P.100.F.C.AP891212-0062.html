[1] The East German Communist Party, through its official newspaper, Neues Deutschland, provided one of the bluntest statements to date concerning the country's troubled situation. [2] It admitted that the East German socialist system had failed, and expressed support for some type of partnership with West Germany. [3] Forty thousand people demonstrated for unification in Karl Marx Stadt, and in Leipzig and Dresden shouting matches broke out between pro and anti-unification demonstrators. [4] The rallies came shortly after the four victorious allies of World War II met in West Berlin to discuss the latest developments in East Germany and Berlin.
