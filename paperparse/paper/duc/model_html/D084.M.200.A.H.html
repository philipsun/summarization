[1] An explosion in the battleship Iowa's No.2 turret on Wednesday, April 19, 1989, during a training exercise NE of Puerto Rico, killed 47. [2] Naval investigators concluded the explosion occurred while gunpowder was in transit from a powder hoist. [3] Crew in upper turret chambers died instantly in the explosion and fire while those at lower levels suffocated as the blast sucked out oxygen. [4] Eleven escaped from powder magazines at the turret's base. [5] On Sunday, 3000 family and friends welcomed the Iowa home to Norfolk. [6] Sailors with black arm bands lined the Iowa's rails and two surviving guncrew members stood on the blackened No.2 turret. [7] On Monday, President Bush spoke to 8000 mourners at a memorial service and Iowa commander Capt. Fred Moosally praised the victims. [8] A moratorium was placed on firing the 16-inch guns, which are criticized as outdated and dangerous, using gunpowder packed in silk bags that are rammed into a gun after the shell and ignited by electric spark. [9] Newer guns' powder is packed in metal containers. [10] Missiles have integrated propellants. [11] The four WWII-era battleships came out of mothballs in 1982. [12] Critics say they have outlived their purpose but the Navy disagrees and is keeping them in service. [13] The Senate Armed Services Committee will hold hearings on the explosion.
