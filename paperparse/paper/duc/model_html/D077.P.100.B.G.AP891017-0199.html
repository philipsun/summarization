[1] A violent earthquake registering 6.9 on the Richter scale rocked Northern California Tuesday at 5:04 pm. [2] One report said the quake was centered near Hollister, 80 miles southeast of San Francisco in San Benito County. [3] It was felt as far away as Reno NV, 225 miles southeast. [4] The extent of the damage was not immediately clear. [5] A section of both the San Francisco Bridge and the Oakland-San Francisco Bridge collapsed. [6] A building and a bridge in downtown Hollister collapsed. [7] A major fire was burning in San Francisco's Marina District. [8] World Series fans in Candlestick Park were terrified as the stands swayed.
