[1] The collapse of the Soviet Union was accompanied by major civil unrest and ethnic conflict in the former republics. [2] The Caucasus, Baltic and Central Asian republics all experienced unrest. [3] The reasons for the strife included nationalism, ethnic tensions and old grievances. [4] One of the most serious conflicts was in the Nagorno-Karabakh region of Azerbaijan. [5] The majority of the population is Armenian and wanted unification with Armenia. [6] The Baltic republics wanted to be independent and many nationalities were demanding the return of lands taken from them under Stalin. [7] In Moldova, the ethnic Romanian majority wanted to establish Moldavian as the official language.
