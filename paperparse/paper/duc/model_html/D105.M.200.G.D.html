[1] The collapse of the Soviet Union was accompanied by major civil unrest and ethnic conflict in the former republics. [2] The Caucasus, Baltic and Central Asian republics all experienced unrest. [3] The reasons for the strife included nationalism, ethnic tensions and old grievances. [4] One of the most serious conflicts was in the Nagorno-Karabakh region of Azerbaijan. [5] The majority of the population there is Armenian and wanted unification with Armenia, Azerbaijan refused. [6] The conflict between the Azeris and the Armenians has existed for centuries. [7] Other conflicts were a direct result of the breakup of the Soviet Union. [8] In Moldova, the ethnic Romanian majority wanted to establish Moldova, which is close to Romanian as the official language. [9] The Russian minority dissented and demanded its own autonomous republic. [10] The Baltic republics wanted to be independent nations and many nationalities were demanding the return of lands taken from them under Stalin. [11] Gorbachev tried to hold the Soviet Union together through tougher legislation and by appeals for support for his reforms. [12] Following the collapse of the Soviet Union there was even an attempt at peacekeeping in Georgia, using Russian and Georgian troops. [13] There was even one call for an international peacekeeping mission in some of the hotspots.
