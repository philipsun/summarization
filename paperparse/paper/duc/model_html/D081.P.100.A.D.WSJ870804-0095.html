[1] The National Union of Mineworkers said an estimated 200,000 gold and coal miners in South Africa will begin a strike for higher wages and better working conditions in what it said would be the largest job action to ever affect the nation's mining industry. [2] Gold and coal account for more than half of South Africa's foreign currency earnings. [3] Mine owners, in anticipation of a strike, have been building stockpiles and do not expect the strike to have much impact unless it lasts over a month. [4] Other mines, such as diamond mines, may go on strike if the current striking miners' demands are not met.
