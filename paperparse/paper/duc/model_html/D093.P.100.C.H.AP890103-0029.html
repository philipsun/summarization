[1] The 30-ton ferry Justo Rufino Barrios II, which sank off Guatemala's east coast Sunday, was authorized to carry 90 passengers but had 120. [2] It ran out of fuel midway on a 16-mile run across Amatique Bay from Livingston to Puerto Barrios. [3] The skipper of a patrol boat towing it to port requested that 22-year-old ferry captain Juan Pablo Ezquibel take control of the ferry's wheel, but Esquibel did so only when the boat began to capsize, after passengers ran to one side when the boat leaned while riding a small wave. [4] Sixty-seven people drowned and 14 are missing. [5] Esquibel was arrested for negligent homicide.
