[1] Hugo, a category 4 hurricane, struck South Carolina Thursday and officials hoped precautions were sufficient. [2] As Hugo approached, roads and resort towns from Hilton Head to Myrtle Beach were nearly empty as coastal residents fled inland and crowded into shelters. [3] Hurricane-force winds first hit land at 9:15pm. [4] Three hours later the 10-mile eye of the storm passed just east of Charleston, with top winds of 135 mph. 119-mph winds in Charleston brought down power lines, snapped trees, exploded utility transformers, and littered the street with tin roofs. [5] Schools and businesses were closed. [6] Hugo was expected to turn north, cause severe flooding, and bring tornadoes.
