[1] Losses to insurers from Hurricane Hugo far surpass the $752.5 million for Hurricane Frederick in 1979, but analysts and company officials predict no increase in premium rates before mid-1990. [2] Mr. Ruoff of Fred S. James figures that even if losses reach $5 billion insurers won't feel enough "financial pain" to make them boost premium rates right away. [3] In the last three years hurricane and storm seasons have been rather mild so insurers haven't had to dip into reserves. [4] Most insurers also protect themselves by buying reinsurance, and even State Farm, which doesn't buy reinsurance, believes its reserves are large enough to absorb its losses.
