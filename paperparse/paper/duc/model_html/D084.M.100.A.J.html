[1] On Monday President Bush and the USS Iowa commander attended a memorial at Norfolk Naval Air Station for 47 sailors that were killed in an explosion of the battleship's No. 2 gun turret last Wednesday. [2] The Iowa, a WW II battleship returned to duty in 1982, was conducting firing exercises near Puerto Rico at the time. [3] The Defense Department said the blast possibly originated with a gunpowder bag used to fire the one-ton shells. [4] Eleven people inside the turret, all on the lowest deck loading powder, survived. [5] The Navy is investigating the accident. [6] Firings of the 16-inch guns have been suspended.
