[1] John Major continues to face leadership challenges as he enters the second year of his term. [2] In an attempt to bolster his support, Cabinet ministers rallied behind Major yesterday, insisting there would be no leadership election in the fall. [3] The public show of support did little to dispel doubts among Tory MPs about Major's ability to stay in office. [4] Their doubts were confirmed in the findings of a national opinion poll held by the Independent On Sunday which indicated that Michael Heseltine was favorite to replace Major. [5] Despite efforts to foster unity in the Conservative Party, Major's chances of surviving have shortened to 11/10 against him.
