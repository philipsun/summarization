[1] Experience of a reporter at Candlestick Park for the World Series the night of the 1989 San Francisco quake: [2] I was sitting in a trailer outside the stadium when the earthquake hit. [3] I looked out and the stadium was shaking. [4] A special concrete joint on top of the stadium was opening and closing like cardboard. [5] A few people were running out. [6] I walked into the stadium to interview fans and sensed little panic. [7] Most people stayed, waiting to hear whether the game would be postponed. [8] When leaving, those in the upper deck seemed more shaken that those in lower levels.
