[1] Britons expressed anger and concern over the lax security procedures at British bases following the Friday IRA bombing at the Royal Marines Music School in Deal. [2] That attack left 10 dead and 22 injured, 12 seriously. [3] The Royal Marines Music School is one of 30 bases in England employing private security firms. [4] Considered a money saving measure, there were calls to review the policy. [5] Because of the tighter security at bases considered to be at high risk, the IRA has turned its attention to bases they consider more vulnerable. [6] The attack came after Prime Minister Thatcher's speech in Ulster, praising the Ulster Defense Regiment.
