[1] The 1988 New York City Marathon drew a field of over 23,000 runners. [2] It was the largest field in the 19-year history of the marathon. [3] Steve Jones of Wales was the man's winner, and Grete Waitz of Norway won the women's division for a record ninth time. [4] Enthusiastic spectators cheered all the participants, but were most interested in friends or family who were participating. [5] More than 13,000 runners from 13 countries and 49 states ran the Marine Corps Marathon in Washington, D.C. [6] Unlike most marathons, the one in Washington pays no prize money to winners.
