[1] The Queen of TV comedy and entrepreneur, Lucille Ball, died on April 26, 1989 at 77. [2] Presidents, comedians and actors, neighbors, residents of her hometown, television critics, surviving co-stars, and just plain fans paid tributes to her. [3] More than 2,000 attended three memorial services. [4] She was a chorus girl, model, and in 75 movies, but is best remembered for her physical and verbal comedy and superb timing evident on television. [5] She and her husband, Desi Arnaz, revolutionized television comedy with "I Love Lucy," which was filmed before a live audience, becoming a rerun gold mine, still seen world-wide.
