[1] Philippine President Corazon Aquino's administration was beset with problems from June 1988 to October 1990. [2] Her predecessor, Ferdinand Marcos was exiled to Hawaii, but had allies in the Philippines and continued to create dissension. [3] Swiss bankers required a criminal prosecution to release information on the $10 billion he allegedly stole and deposited in Switzerland. [4] After he became terminally ill, his wife, Imelda, and his supporters wanted him to be allowed to die, or at least be buried, in his homeland. [5] Aquino refused all requests citing security reasons. [6] From a landlord family, Aquino's agrarian reform policy suffered a number of setbacks. [7] Economic problems were the focus of her 1989 European trip, where she touted improvements in the growth rate under her leadership and sought debt relief and foreign investments. [8] The Philippine military created a continuing problem, leading coups and mutinies. [9] The December 1989 attempted coup divided Filipinos in Los Angeles and involved Aquino asking for help from United States forces stationed there. [10] Criticized for past indecisive actions, Aquino quickly ended the October 1990 coup attempt on Mindanao. [11] Communist insurgents continued their 21-year campaign. [12] In April 1990, she said she was willing to negotiate with the insurgents and renegade military.
