[1] Lucille Ball, who died in April 1989 at the age of 77, will long be remembered. [2] She made her craft look easy, spontaneous and unrehearsed. [3] The result was the durable and probably the finest series in television history, "I Love Lucy". [4] She starred with her husband, Desi Arnaz, in the '50s show and together they founded Desilu Productions, which eventually took over RKO Studios. [5] She was an astute businesswoman as well as a talented entertainer and Desilu produced some of the most popular programs of the '50s and '60s. [6] Ball and Arnaz divorced in 1960. [7] They had two children. [8] At her death there was an outpouring of sympathy from the very famous to thousands of her ordinary fans. [9] She was particularly remembered in her hometown of Jamestown New York where she had contributed to local charities, civic events and Jamestown Community College. [10] Following the breakup of her first marriage, she married Gary Morton, her husband at her death. [11] Miss Ball had a successful career before television, starring in 75 films. [12] She died in Los Angeles, following emergency heart surgery. [13] William Paley, Chairman of CBS said that she helped to establish television as the nation's leading entertainment industry and that her legacy would last forever.
