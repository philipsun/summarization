[1] The engineer of a ferry that sank off a Red Sea port, leaving more than 400 people missing, denied accusations of widespread negligence on the part of the ship's crew. [2] Investigators were looking into complaints from some survivors that the crew abandoned the ferry without helping passengers and that some crew members pushed passengers aside to get to the lifeboats. [3] The ship's engineer acknowledged misconduct by some crew members, but said the survival of only 10 of the 71 member crew, indicated negligence was not widespread. [4] The ferry hit a reef and quickly sank. [5] The ferry was returning to Egypt from Jiddah, Saudi Arabia.
