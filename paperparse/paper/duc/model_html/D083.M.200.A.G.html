[1] A powerful storm system brought tornadoes, high winds, heavy thunderstorms, and large hailstones across the Midwest, Mississippi Valley, Southeast, and Northeast on Wednesday and Thursday. [2] Reportedly, 27 people were killed, more than 500 injured, and hundreds left homeless. [3] The storms developed ahead of a strong cold front sweeping eastward from Missouri and Arkansas. [4] Tornadoes were reported in Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, New Jersey, Maryland, Delaware, North Carolina, Georgia, Alabama, and Mississippi. [5] The worst damage was in Huntsville, AL where a tornado laid waste a wide swath of the city and caused the death of 14 people. [6] Four people were killed by a tornado in Palmetto, Georgia. [7] Sixty-five mph winds damaged Kentucky and Louisiana. [8] Strong winds and heavy thunderstorms caused heavy damage in Illinois, the District of Columbia, Maryland, Delaware, New Jersey, New York, Pennsylvania, and West Virginia. [9] In Newburgh, New York, a tree crashed through a school roof killing at least seven children. [10] One person was killed in Elizabeth, New Jersey and another in New York City. [11] Thunderstorms dropped large hailstones in Indiana and Tennessee. [12] Snow was falling from the northern Rockies to the Great Lakes. [13] Storm warnings were also posted in Pennsylvania and cold weather was expected in the Southeast.
