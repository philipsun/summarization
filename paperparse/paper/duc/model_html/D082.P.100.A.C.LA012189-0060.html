[1] Soviet physicists, in a rebuff to the Soviet Union's scientific establishment, voted on Friday to nominate Andrei Sakharov to be a candidate in the March parliamentary elections. [2] The overwhelming vote, taken in secrecy, came only two days after the Governing Board of the Academy of Sciences rejected Sakharov's candidacy. [3] The vote was more an expression of respect for Sakharov than a specific endorsement of his political views. [4] Sakharov used the occasion to outline a detailed program that would transform the Soviet Union. [5] Complicated voting procedures still make it uncertain that Sakharov's name will appear on the ballot.
