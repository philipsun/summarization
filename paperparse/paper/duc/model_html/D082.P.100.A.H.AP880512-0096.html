[1] Andrei D. Sakharov, 66, won the 1975 Nobel Peace Prize. [2] He was exiled to Gorky for seven years after criticizing the Soviet drive into Afghanistan, returning to Moscow with his wife Yelena Bonner in December 1986. [3] Sakharov belongs to the Academy of Sciences and helped create the Soviet hydrogen bomb. [4] He vocally supports Gorbachev's reform campaign but calls for political prisoner liberation and human rights improvements. [5] Past Soviet reports called the couple traitors, accusing Bonner of marrying Sakharov for his money, and saying they lived in luxury amid scandals. [6] Now press says they live simply. [7] Sakharov always does the dishes. [8] His apartment is a mecca for Soviets seeking his aid.
