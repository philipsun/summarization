[1] Hurricane Hugo, packing winds of up to 135 mph, raged into Charleston Thursday night. [2] Dubbed a "killer hurricane" by Mayor Joseph Riley Jr., it is the first major hurricane to hit the 300-year-old city since 1959. [3] The worst disaster to strike the city in recent decades was a tornado in 1938 that killed 32 people and injured hundreds. [4] The worst natural disaster to beset the city ever occurred on Aug. 32, 1886, when an earthquake measuring 7.7 on the Richter scale struck, claiming 110 lives and destroying 102 buildings.
