[1] Andrei Sakharov was banished without trial to the closed city of Gorky in January 1980. [2] He was allowed to return to Moscow in December 1986. [3] Now, in May 1988, the Moscow News has published an article that makes a clear attempt to refute some of the charges made by Soviet officials and the state-controlled media against Sakharov and his wife. [4] Sakharov has supported Gorbachev's reforms, but has pressed Gorbachev for speedier progress in improving the Soviet record on human rights. [5] He recently handed Gorbachev a list of 200 people who were imprisoned for their opinions.
