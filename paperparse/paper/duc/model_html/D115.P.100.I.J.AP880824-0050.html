[1] The death toll from Sunday's Himalayan earthquake rose to over 549 in Nepal as reports arrive from remote areas. [2] Blocked or severely damaged roads and roads flooded by monsoon rains have severely hampered relief efforts. [3] Dharan, near Katmandu, was the hardest hit area. [4] There, over 131 victims were found, most in unstable brick and mud homes. [5] Police Inspector Adhikari said bulldozers razed over 50 severely damaged houses. [6] Prime Minister Shresta called the disaster, "the biggest calamity to strike Nepal". [7] Indian officials claim the death toll is 200 or less, but opposition politicians and the press say 400 to 1500 perished.
