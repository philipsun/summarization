[1] Ronald Spiers, a veteran US diplomat will be the new undersecretary-general for General Assembly affairs at the UN. [2] Secretary-General Javier Perez de Cuellar announced the appointment. [3] This is the top ranking American post at the world body and Spiers will replace Joseph Reed, a banker who served in that post until since 1983. [4] Reed will become chief of protocol for the United States. [5] Spiers is a career foreign service officer and has been ambassador to the Bahamas and Turkey and, from 1981 until 1983 was ambassador to Pakistan. [6] Spiers most recent job was undersecretary of state for management affairs.
