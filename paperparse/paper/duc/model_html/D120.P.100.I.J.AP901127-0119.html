[1] John Major, 47, Margaret Thatcher's protege, was elected Conservative Party leader Tuesday night and will succeed her as prime minister. [2] Mrs. Thatcher, ending 11 years in power, intended to submit her resignation to Queen Elizabeth II on Wednesday. [3] Major would then be called to lead the government. [4] His election climaxed a tumultuous month for the Conservative Party. [5] The party trailed the Labor Party in the polls and lost a string of local Parliamentary elections. [6] On Nov. 1, Deputy Prime Minister, Sir Geoffrey Howe resigned in a policy dispute over the European Community, calling Mrs. Thatcher a threat to Britain's vital interests.
