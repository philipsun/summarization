[1] Hurricane Hugo struck South Carolina Thursday after thousands of coastal residents evacuated inland. [2] In Charleston Hugo littered the streets with tin roofs, palmetto fronds and chunks of bark and by midnight 119 mph winds cracked the roof of the 188-year-old City Hall that had withstood several earlier hurricanes. [3] About 12,000 people had checked into 83 shelters across the state as of Thursday evening. [4] A spokesman for the Red Cross reported that they were providing shelter for tens of thousands of people all along the eastern seaboard as well as for 25,000 in the Caribbean. [5] Hugo is expected to turn north causing severe flooding up the East Coast.
