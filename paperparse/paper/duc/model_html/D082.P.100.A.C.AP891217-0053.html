[1] Sunday was the first day of memorial ceremonies for Andrei Sakharov. [2] As many as 70,000 mourners viewed his body in the Youth Palace. [3] Sub-zero temperatures prevailed, but the mourners waited in a line that stretched a mile from the Palace. [4] It was a vast outpouring of grief for a man once ostracized and sent into exile. [5] It was as if the populace was apologizing for being late in recognizing his contributions. [6] He will be buried on Monday. [7] Yelena Bonner, his widow and fellow activist, said that she hopes his writings will finally be made widely available in his homeland.
