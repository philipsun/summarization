[1] Troops of the former Soviet Union were ordered out of the Armenian enclave of Nagorno-Karabakh after Armenian and Azeri militants fired on them. [2] The enclave is administered by Azerbaijan, but largely inhabited by Armenians. [3] Armenians in the enclave want unification with Armenia, but Azerbaijan refuses to give up the territory. [4] The commander of the Soviet troops has long warned that he would pull out troops rather than to allow them to become the targets of ethnic conflicts. [5] Troops under fire along the Armenian-Azerbaijan border will also be withdrawn. [6] Azeri and Armenian officials say that guerrillas on both sides of the conflict are using sophisticated weapons.
