[1] John Major was elected to succeed Margaret Thatcher as prime minister Tuesday night by the Conservative party. [2] Major fell two votes short of a majority but his two opponents quickly conceded defeat and Major was confirmed as the winner. [3] Major succeeds following an initial challenge to party leadership by former Defense Secretary Michael Heseltine last week. [4] When it became apparent that she could not defeat the Heseltine challenge, Mrs. Thatcher resigned in order to let someone from the Cabinet stand against Heseltine. [5] Major and Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd both joined the race, a race in which Thatcher endorsed Major.
