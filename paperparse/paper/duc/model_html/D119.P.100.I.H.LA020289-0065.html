[1] President Bush is nominating former CIA officer James R. Lilley as ambassador to China, to replace Winston Lord, who has headed the embassy in Beijing since 1985. [2] Lilley, 60, was CIA station chief in China when Bush headed the US liaison office there in 1974-75. [3] He was born in China, the son of an American oilman. [4] He served on the NSC staff, headed the US office in Taiwan, and was deputy assistant secretary of state for East Asia. [5] For the last three years, Lilley has been ambassador to South Korea and hoped to become assistant secretary of state for US policy in East Asia.
